# Keyword Capitalization Guide

This document defines the canonical capitalization for all mechanical terms in the Heroic Fantasy RPG system. Use this as the authoritative reference when writing or editing game text.

---

## Core Mechanics

**Actions (Always Capitalized):**
- Clash
- Defy
- Talk
- Sense
- Know

**Approaches (Always Capitalized):**
- Daring
- Grace
- Charm
- Cunning
- Spirit

**Outcomes (Always Capitalized):**
- Triumph
- Success
- Conflict
- Setback
- Disaster

**Effects (Context-Dependent):**
- Primary Effect (when referring to the system concept)
- Special Effect (when referring to Trait invocations)
- Enhanced Effect (Special Effect category)
- Secondary Effect (Special Effect type - free Defy action)
- Greater Effect (specific Enhanced Effect type)
- Broad Effect (specific Enhanced Effect type)
- Effects (lowercase when general usage)

---

## Dice & Modifiers

**Always Capitalized:**
- Boon (advantage mechanic)
- Bane (disadvantage mechanic)
- Edge (Complication negation)

**Context-Dependent:**
- Expertise (capitalized when referring to the mechanical feature)
- expertise (lowercase when general skill/mastery)

---

## Resources & Tracking

**Always Capitalized:**
- Stress (the resource/track)
- Momentum (the metacurrency)
- Condition (the mechanical status)
- Burden (persistent Condition)
- Consequence (mechanical outcome)

**Track Types (Always Capitalized):**
- Harm (track type)
- Will (track type)
- Obstacle (track type)

**Context-Dependent:**
- Taken Out (capitalized when referring to the mechanical state)
- taken out (lowercase in casual narrative usage)

---

## Challenge Mechanics

**Always Capitalized:**
- Challenge (when referring to mechanical opposition)
- Complication (the mechanical tag/feature)
- Challenge Move (enemy ability type)
- Impact Move (GM move type)
- Challenge Rating / CR (rating system)
- Rank (power/scope tier)

**Complication Tags (Always Capitalized):**
- Resilient
- Warded
- Elusive
- Vigilant
- Complex
- Resolute
- Legendary
- Deadly

## Character Features

**Always Capitalized:**
- Trait (character descriptors)
- Talent (mechanical abilities)
- Calling (character archetype)
- Core Talent (Calling-defining ability)
- Additional Talent (supplementary abilities)

**Specific Callings (Always Capitalized):**
- Champion
- Hunter
- Mystic
- Sage
- Shadow
- Vanguard

## Magic & Spellcasting

**Always Capitalized:**
- Sphere (magical discipline)
- Operation (spell action type)
- Spell Theorem (known spell)
- Arcane Formula (improvised spell mechanic)
- Cantrip (spell magnitude)
- Spell (spell magnitude, when referring to the tier)
- Potent Spell (spell magnitude)
- Ritual (spell category)
- Favor (Mystic resource for patron relationship)
- Domain (Mystic patron purview)
- Patron (Mystic spiritual entity)

**Spheres (Always Capitalized):**
- Matter
- Energy
- Mind
- Spirit
- Space
- Time
- Pattern

**Context-Dependent:**
- spell (lowercase when referring to a specific spell cast, not the magnitude tier)
- ritual (lowercase when general ceremonial activity)

---

## Rest & Recovery

**Always Capitalized:**
- Short Rest (1 hour recovery)
- Long Rest (overnight recovery)

**Context-Dependent:**
- rest (lowercase when general concept)

---

## Combat & Positioning

**Always Capitalized:**
- Close (range band)
- Near (range band)
- Far (range band)

**Lowercase:**
- range (general distance concept)

---

## GM Tools

**Always Capitalized:**
- Vantage (fictional positioning / difficulty system)
- Stakes (consequence severity calibration)
- Soft Move (minor GM consequence)
- Hard Move (major GM consequence)  
- Suspense Move (setup / warning move)
- Impact Move (consequence / follow-through move)
- Death Move (enemy defeated ability)
- Countdown (track type)

**Vantage Tiers (Always Capitalized):**
- Certain (auto-success)
- Standard (standard roll)
- Difficult (roll with Banes)
- Impossible (cannot attempt)

**Stakes Levels (Always Capitalized):**
- Low Stakes (reduce Consequence Tier)
- Regular Stakes (standard consequences)
- High Stakes (increase Consequence Tier)

**Context-Dependent:**
- vantage (lowercase when referring to general advantage or positioning, not the system)

---

## Special Mechanics

**Always Capitalized:**
- Create an Opening (Momentum spend)
- Resist Consequence (Momentum spend)
- Assist (helping action)
- Setup (advantage creation)
- Teamwork (group action)
- Build Momentum (Special Effect)
- Push Yourself (Talent ability trigger phrase)
- Tangle (personal complication for Momentum)

---

## Context-Dependent Terms

These terms should be capitalized **only when referring to the mechanical game concept**, not in general usage:

**Capitalize when mechanical:**
- "When you mark Stress..." (the resource)
- "The knight has Expertise with swords" (the mechanical feature)
- "Roll Clash using Daring" (the Action and Approach)
- "You gain 1 Boon" (the modifier)
- "The enemy has Resilient" (the Complication tag)
- "You suffer a Consequence" (the mechanical outcome)
- "Make a Short Rest" (the recovery mechanic)
- "The Challenge has Will 6" (the mechanical opponent)
- "Your Trait is relevant" (character feature)

**Lowercase when general:**
- "She clashed swords with him" (narrative action)
- "He spoke with great charm" (personality trait)
- "The conflict escalated" (narrative situation, not outcome)
- "This is a setback for our plans" (narrative problem, not outcome)
- "She has expertise in swordplay" (general skill)
- "They rested for an hour" (general downtime)
- "The challenge was difficult" (general obstacle)

---

## Numbers & Tiers

**Always Written as:**
- "Rank 1" / "Rank 2" / "Rank 3" / "Rank 4" (not Rank I, Rank One)
- "CR 1" / "CR 2" / "CR 3" / "CR 4" (not CR I, Tier 1)
- "Condition tier 1/2/3" (lowercase "tier" when describing levels)
- "Burden tier 2" (lowercase "tier")
- "2 ticks" / "4 ticks" / "6 ticks" / "8 ticks"
- "1 Stress" / "2 Stress" (not "1 stress", not "2 stresses")
- "1 Momentum" (not "1 momentum")
- "1 Boon" / "2 Banes" (singular/plural as appropriate)

---

## Abbreviations

**Accepted abbreviations:**
- CR (Challenge Rating) — always capitalized
- PC (Player Character) — always capitalized
- NPC (Non-Player Character) — always capitalized
- GM (Game Master) — always capitalized
- XP (Experience Points) — always capitalized

**Do NOT abbreviate:**
- Complications (not "Comps")
- Momentum (not "Mom")
- Expertise (not "Exp")

## Common Errors to Avoid

❌ **Wrong:** "You rolled a conflict outcome"  
✅ **Correct:** "You rolled a Conflict outcome"

❌ **Wrong:** "The enemy has the resilient trait"  
✅ **Correct:** "The enemy has the Resilient Complication"

❌ **Wrong:** "Spend momentum to create an opening"  
✅ **Correct:** "Spend Momentum to Create an Opening"

❌ **Wrong:** "You gain a boon from your ally's setup"  
✅ **Correct:** "You gain a Boon from your ally's Setup"

❌ **Wrong:** "Roll defy using cunning"  
✅ **Correct:** "Roll Defy using Cunning"

❌ **Wrong:** "The knight is Rank 2 and has a harm track of 4 ticks"  
✅ **Correct:** "The knight is Rank 2 and has a Harm track of 4 ticks"

❌ **Wrong:** "When you take a short rest"  
✅ **Correct:** "When you take a Short Rest"

❌ **Wrong:** "This is standard difficulty"  
✅ **Correct:** "This is Standard Vantage" or "Your Vantage is Standard"

❌ **Wrong:** "The stakes are high"  
✅ **Correct:** "This is High Stakes" (when referring to the mechanical system)

---

## Editorial Notes

**When in doubt:** Capitalize mechanical terms that appear in stat blocks, character sheets, or rule explanations. Lowercase terms used narratively or descriptively.

**Consistency check:** If the word appears on a character sheet or in a Challenge stat block, it should be capitalized in rules text.

**Fiction vs. Mechanics:** "She clashed with the guard" (fiction) vs. "Roll Clash to attack" (mechanics).
