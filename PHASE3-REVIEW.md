## Item #15: Champion Talent Redundancy

**Editor concern:** Battleborn, Defy Death, Iron Will, and Tough as Nails all answer "how do I keep going when things go badly?" without clear distinctions.

**Current wording:**

**Battleborn [ ] [ ]**
> You gain 1 Edge when you Defy in battle. Mark this to Push Yourself in battle without spending Stress, or to Resist a Consequence caused by physical harm, exhaustion, or environmental danger without spending Momentum.

**Defy Death [ ] [ ]**
> When you would be Taken Out, you may instead mark this to remain in the fight with 1 Stress remaining. Push Yourself to stay standing through a consequence that would floor anyone else.

**Iron Will**
> You have Expertise when Defying fear, coercion, temptation, or any attempt to break your resolve. Push Yourself to completely ignore an effect that would compel, dominate, or subvert your will.

**Tough as Nails**
> When you clear a Condition or Burden tier through action or rest, reduce it by 2 tiers instead of 1. Push Yourself to act at full capacity despite a Condition or Burden that would disable lesser warriors — fighting on a broken leg, moving through agony, running through collapse.

### Analysis

**Mechanical distinctions ARE clear:**
- **Battleborn:** Proactive positioning (1 Edge on Defy in battle, prevent costs)
- **Defy Death:** Emergency failsafe (when Taken Out → stay at 1 Stress)
- **Iron Will:** Mental/social defense only (Expertise vs fear/coercion/compulsion)
- **Tough as Nails:** Recovery acceleration + Push through disability

**Thematic overlap:** All are about endurance/toughness, which is the Champion's identity.

**Fiction clarity:** Each already has distinguishing phrases:
- Battleborn mentions "proactive" nature through "Push without spending Stress"
- Defy Death is explicitly reactive: "when you would be Taken Out"
- Iron Will scopes to psychological: "fear, coercion, temptation"
- Tough as Nails focuses on recovery speed and fighting through injury

### Recommendation

**NO CHANGES NEEDED.** The talents have clear mechanical distinctions and the fiction already differentiates them. The thematic overlap is intentional — Champions ARE about endurance and pushing through adversity. Having multiple talents in this space gives players meaningful choices about HOW their Champion endures.

**If clarification is desired (optional):**
Could add one-line flavor summaries:
- Battleborn: *(Battle-hardened veteran who shrugs off what would slow others)*
- Defy Death: *(Too stubborn to die)*
- Iron Will: *(Unbreakable mind, even if the body fails)*
- Tough as Nails: *(Recovers faster and fights through crippling wounds)*

But this feels unnecessary — the descriptions are already clear.

## Item #16: Master Tracker / Wilderness Ghost Distinction

**Editor concern:** Both occupy adjacent stealth/tracking space without clear differentiation.

**Current wording:**

**Master Tracker**
> You have 1 Edge when tracking. When you successfully track a target, also learn one additional detail about them (how many, how long ago, their condition, their destination). Push Yourself to track something that should be impossible to follow.

**Wilderness Ghost**
> You have Expertise when moving unseen, leaving no trace, or camouflaging yourself in natural terrain. Push Yourself to extend this to your entire group — concealing allies, erasing their trail, or hiding everyone in plain sight using the terrain itself.

### Analysis

**These ARE distinct:**
- **Master Tracker:** Pursuit and information gathering (following someone, learning about them)

One is offense (hunting), one is defense (hiding).

**Current wording is clear:** Master Tracker says "tracking" and "follow." Wilderness Ghost says "moving unseen" and "camouflaging."

### Recommendation

**NO CHANGES NEEDED.** The talents are clearly differentiated. Master Tracker = hunter, Wilderness Ghost = infiltrator.

**Optional clarification (if desired):**
Could add explicit scope notes, but current wording is sufficient:
- Master Tracker: *(Pursuit specialist)*
- Wilderness Ghost: *(Concealment specialist)*

## Item #17: Lightning Reflexes / Opportunist Combo

**Editor concern:** Lightning Reflexes (always act first) + Opportunist (exploit openings) = always From the Shadows bonus?

**Current wording:**

**Lightning Reflexes**
> You can always act first in a conflict unless completely surprised. Push Yourself to act first even when surprised.

**Opportunist**
> When you exploit a weakness, distraction, or opening, you can perform an action that would normally require extensive setup — lifting a key mid-conversation, planting evidence during a search, slipping past a guard in the moment they look away.

**From the Shadows (Shadow Core Talent):**
> When you act from a position of advantage — concealment, surprise, or superior knowledge — your action gains 1 Boon and 1 Edge.

### Analysis

**Does combo = always From the Shadows?**

**No.** Acting first ≠ acting from advantage:
- Lightning Reflexes = initiative order (who goes first)
- From the Shadows requires "position of advantage" = concealment, surprise, or superior knowledge
- Acting first in open combat doesn't grant concealment or surprise after first round

**Opportunist doesn't grant advantage either:**
- It allows actions that normally need setup (narrative permission)
- Doesn't explicitly grant "position of advantage" trigger

**Combo is strong but not automatic:**
- First round of combat from stealth: Yes, From the Shadows applies (Lightning Reflexes + surprise)
- Subsequent rounds: No automatic From the Shadows unless Shadow maintains concealment
- Opportunist situations: Only if the opening creates "position of advantage" (GM discretion)

### Recommendation

**NO CHANGES NEEDED.** The combo is strong (2-talent investment should be strong) but not automatic. GM adjudication of "position of advantage" prevents abuse.

**Flag for playtesting:** Monitor whether this combo proves problematic in actual play. Current wording doesn't require errata.

**Editor concern:** This pattern is buried mid-Chapter 7. It's one of the most immediately useful GM tools and deserves prominent visual treatment.

**Current location:** Chapter 7, "Vigilance" section (around line 388+)

### Analysis

The Hint → Reveal → Strike pattern is indeed powerful and frequently referenced. Making it visually prominent would improve usability.

### Recommendation

**CREATE CALLOUT BOX** around the existing text in Vigilance section.

**Proposed format:**
```markdown
> ## Hint → Reveal → Strike Pattern
>
> **The three-step escalation for telegraphing danger:**
>
> 1. **Hint (Soft):** "You notice scratch marks on the floor"
> 2. **Reveal (Suspense):** "The marks lead to a pressure plate — your foot is inches away"
> 3. **Strike (Impact):** "The plate clicks. Poison darts shoot from the walls!"
>
> Give players chance to respond at each step. Strike only if they ignore warnings or fail to stop the threat.
```
