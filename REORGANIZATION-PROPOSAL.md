# Book Reorganization Proposal

**Status:** Proposed (not yet implemented)  
**Date:** 2026-06-27  
**Rationale:** Address rules drift, repetition, and unclear reader paths by organizing by reader need and moment of use

---

## Design Constraints First

Before proposing structure, what does this game need its layout to do?

**For players:**
- Learn the core loop in one sitting
- Build a character without reading the whole book
- Find their Calling and understand it completely
- Reference rules mid-session without hunting across chapters

**For GMs:**
- Understand the full system before running
- Find mid-session answers in seconds
- Design challenges and encounters with clear tools
- Run the game without memorizing everything

**For the book as an artifact:**
- Minimize cross-chapter dependencies
- Every mechanic has exactly one home
- Players and GMs have clearly separate reading paths
- The reference layer is distinct from the teaching layer

---

## The Core Insight

Most RPG books are organized by **mechanic type** (combat chapter, magic chapter, equipment chapter). This game should be organized by **reader need** and **moment of use**.

Three distinct moments of use:

1. **Learning** — reading before play, understanding the system
2. **Creating** — building a character, making choices
3. **Playing** — mid-session lookup, quick reference

Current structure tries to serve all three simultaneously in every chapter. The ideal structure separates them.

## Proposed Structure

---

### PART 0: BEFORE YOU PLAY
*Read this first. Both players and GM.*

---

**Welcome**
Half a page. What kind of game this is, who it's for, what makes it distinctive. Not rules. Pure orientation.

**The Core Loop**
Two pages. The six-step sequence from "GM frames situation" to "GM narrates result." One worked example of play showing the whole loop. No mechanical detail — just the shape of how a session works. A new player should finish this and know what playing feels like.

---

### PART 1: THE RULES
*The complete mechanical system. Organized by how mechanics relate to each other, not by chapter tradition.*

---

**Chapter 1: Rolling Dice**

Everything about the resolution engine itself, separated from what actions do.

- The dice pool (Approach + Boons - Banes)
- The four outcomes (Setback / Conflict / Success / Triumph)
- Boon and Bane sources and the ±2d cap
- Edge — what it is, how it differs from Boon
- Expertise — what it is, when it triggers, the Setback → Conflict conversion
- Probability tables
- Pool minimum (2d take lowest)

*Nothing about specific actions, nothing about characters. Pure resolution engine.*

**Chapter 2: Taking Action**

What the five actions do and how they integrate with the resolution engine.

- The five Actions and what each one accomplishes on success
- The five Approaches and what each one represents
- How Actions and Approaches combine
- Vantage — the four tiers (Certain / Strong / Standard / Limited / Impossible), what each means, how the GM sets it
- Stakes — the three levels (Low / Regular / High), what each means, how the GM sets it
- When to roll vs when to say yes
- The complete resolution sequence from declaration to outcome

*Vantage and Stakes live here as integral parts of resolution, not as standalone appendices. Their full reference versions still exist as reference documents but their canonical rules home is here.*

**Chapter 3: Consequences**

What happens after the roll. The complete consequence framework.

- Consequence tiers (Minor / Major / Severe / Disaster)
- How Complications reduce outcomes
- How Stakes modify consequence tiers
- The five specific Action outcomes (what Defy produces, what Clash produces, etc.)
- Progress tracks — Harm, Will, Obstacle, Countdown — their sizes and what fills them
- Enemy actions and Impact Moves
- Resist Consequence — canonical definition here, nowhere else

*This is the chapter that explains what rolling actually produces. It sits between "how to roll" and "how to survive what rolling produces."*

**Chapter 4: Resilience**

The complete system for surviving consequences.

- Stress track — 5 boxes, what fills it, what it represents
- Conditions — naming, tiers, the Bane they apply, how they clear
- Burdens — how they differ from Conditions, recovery methods
- Stress Overflow — the Burden or Taken Out choice
- Taken Out — what it means, how characters recover
- Push Yourself — canonical definition here: mark 1 Stress for 1 Boon on your own roll
- Assist — canonical definition here: mark 1 Stress for 1 Boon on an ally's roll
- Short Rest and Long Rest

*Push Yourself and Assist live here because both cost Stress. This closes the gap that exists throughout the current document set.*

**Chapter 5: Momentum**

Momentum as its own chapter because it's the most important player-facing resource and currently the most scattered.

- What Momentum represents
- How to earn it (Triumph, Build Momentum Trait effect, Tangles)
- The cap (2, raised to 3 by Talent)
- Create an Opening — canonical definition here, nowhere else: spend 1 Momentum for 1 Boon and 1 Edge
- Resist Consequence cross-reference (defined in Chapter 3, referenced here)
- Tangles — defined here as the optional Momentum earn mechanism, flagged as optional

*Momentum gets its own chapter because it currently has no home — it's defined partially in Chapter 1, partially in Chapter 2, partially in Chapter 4, referenced everywhere. One chapter eliminates all of that drift.*

**Chapter 6: Traits**

The complete Trait system.

- What Traits are and what they do
- The three Trait types (Origin, Profession, Personal)
- When to invoke a Trait (Conflict or better, narratively relevant)
- The three Special Effects (Greater Effect, Secondary Effect, Build Momentum)
- Secondary Effect framework — what Defy can do (Take Something, Seize Control, Create Advantage, Change Situation)
- Trait breadth guidance — how broad is too broad, how narrow is too narrow
- One Trait per roll rule

*Traits get their own chapter because they're a distinct mechanical layer that sits on top of the resolution engine. Currently they're split between Chapters 3 and 4.*

### PART 2: YOUR CHARACTER
*Read this when building or advancing your character.*

---

**Chapter 7: Character Creation**

A clean sequential process with no mechanical explanation — just decision points with cross-references to Part 1 for rules detail.

1. Choose your Approaches (3/2/1/1/0)
2. Write your three Traits (Origin, Profession, Personal)
3. Choose your Calling
4. Choose 2 additional Talents
5. Record Stress (5 boxes), Momentum cap (2), starting Rank (1)

*This chapter is a guided process, not a rules chapter. It assumes Part 1 has been read.*

**Chapter 8: Advancement**

Everything about growing your character.

- The advancement table (levels 1-10)
- Approach increases and caps
- Talent acquisition
- Rank thresholds and what each Rank means narratively
- The four Ranks (Local Heroes / Seasoned Adventurers / Regional Legends / Mythic Heroes) with Genre/Scale/Tone descriptions
- Talent retraining
- Cross-Calling Talents and multiclassing
- Re-Skinning Talents

*When to Award Levels is NOT here — that's GM content and lives in Part 4.*

**Chapter 9: Callings**

All six Callings with complete Talent lists.

- Brief introduction to what Callings are
- Each Calling: identity description, archetype range, Core Talent, Additional Talents
- Push Yourself cross-reference for every Talent that uses it
- Design Notes: Callings vs Classes, Expertise domains
- Quick Reference table

*Identical to current Chapter 9 but with Push Yourself defined (via cross-reference to Chapter 4), typos fixed, and Champion Talent differentiators added.*

**Chapter 10: Spellcasting**

Complete spellcasting rules.

- How spellcasting integrates with the resolution engine
- Touchstones — Spheres and Domains
- The seven Spheres with Can/Cannot (Energy sub-types preserved, Pattern condensed)
- The nine Operations
- Example Theorems (five per Sphere)
- Domain examples with Magnitude (four domains, Cantrip/Spell/Potent per domain)
- Magnitude tiers — Cantrip, Spell, Potent Spell, Ritual
- Potent Spell enhancements (Greater Effect, Affect Multiple Targets, Increased Duration, Secondary Consequence)
- Default durations
- Magic and Rank table
- Common rulings (Magical Defense, Healing, Resurrection, Buffs/Debuffs, Detecting Magic, Countering Magic, Potions)
- Rituals (Source, Anchors, Rites, worked example)
- Design Notes

*Substantially the same as current Chapter 10 but with Summary cut, duplicate Potent examples cut, and all rules drift fixed.*

## What This Achieves

**Every mechanic has exactly one home:**
- Resist Consequence → Chapter 3
- Push Yourself → Chapter 4
- Assist → Chapter 4
- Create an Opening → Chapter 5
- Tangles → Chapter 5 (rules) + Chapter 14 (GM guidance)
- Secondary Effects → Chapter 6
- Seize Spotlight → Chapter 15
- When to Award Levels → Chapter 16
- Social Conflict → Chapter 14
- Boss Design → Chapter 13
- Vantage → Vantage Reference (Part 3)
- Stakes → Stakes Reference (Part 3)

**Clear reading paths:**
- New player: Part 0 → Part 1 → Part 2 (your Calling + spellcasting if relevant)
- New GM: Everything, in order
- Experienced player mid-session: Part 3 reference documents
- GM mid-session: GM Cheat Sheet → Part 3 → relevant Part 4 chapter

**Repetition eliminated structurally** because each mechanic's canonical home is designated and all other references point there rather than restating.

**Examples separated from rules** so both are better — rules chapters are shorter and more scannable, examples chapter is richer and more coherent as a teaching resource.

**GM and player content cleanly separated** at the Part level, not just the chapter level.

---

## The One Open Question

Part 1 (The Rules) is still shared between players and GMs. An alternative would be splitting it — a Player Rules section (Chapters 1-4) and a separate Complete Rules section that adds the GM-facing resolution detail. But this risks duplicating the core resolution engine, which is exactly what we're trying to avoid.

The better solution is probably a reading guide at the start — "Players: read Chapters 1-6 and Part 2. GMs: read everything." Simple, honest, no duplication.

## Implementation Notes

**This is a major restructuring effort that would involve:**
1. Creating new chapter files with the proposed structure
2. Migrating content from current chapters to new homes
3. Removing all duplicate content (keeping only canonical versions)
4. Adding cross-references where mechanics reference each other
5. Creating the new Momentum chapter from scattered content
6. Consolidating examples into Appendix B
7. Testing the reading paths with fresh readers

**Estimated scope:** Large (multiple sessions of focused work)

**Priority:** Medium-High (addresses core structural issues causing rules drift)
