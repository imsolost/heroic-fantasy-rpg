## Naming Conventions

### Capitalization
- **Archon** (always capitalized when referring to the game)
- **Core Rules** (capitalized when referring to the rulebook title)
- **Quick Start** (capitalized when referring to the product)

### Mechanics (In-Game Terms)
Capitalize when referring to specific game mechanics:
- Actions: Defy, Clash, Talk, Sense, Know
- Approaches: Daring, Grace, Charm, Cunning, Spirit
- Outcomes: Setback, Conflict, Success, Triumph
- Resources: Stress, Momentum, Edge
- Character Elements: Trait, Calling, Talent, Rank
- Mechanics: Boon, Bane, Complication, Vantage, Stakes
- Progress Tracks: Harm, Will, Obstacle, Countdown
- Other: Expertise, Push Yourself, Assist, Create an Opening, Resist Consequence

Do NOT capitalize when used generically:
- "You have vantage in this situation" (lowercase - generic usage)
- "Assess your Vantage" (capitalized - referring to the mechanic)

## Brand Voice

### Tone
- **Direct and clear** — No jargon, no fluff
- **Confident** — "You do this," not "You might consider doing this"
- **Inspiring** — Emphasize heroism, bold action, dramatic moments
- **Accessible** — Assume no prior RPG knowledge

### What Archon Is
- Heroic fantasy
- Fiction-first
- Momentum-driven
- Dramatic and cinematic
- Player-facing mechanics

### What Archon Is Not
- Gritty realism (heroes can fail, but they're competent)
- Rules-heavy simulation
- Prep-intensive
- Adversarial (GM vs players)

### Logo Concepts
- Strong, angular lettering
- Could incorporate: sword, crown, archway, heroic silhouette

- **Primary:** Deep blue/indigo (authority, heroism)

- **Alternative:** Steel gray and ember orange (steel and fire)

### Typography
- **Headers:** Bold, strong, slightly condensed
- **Body:** Clear, readable, medium weight
- **Callouts:** Italics or alternate weight for emphasis

## Key Design Decisions

### Dice Pool Minimum (2026-06-29)
**Rule:** If your dice pool is 0 or less, roll 2 dice and take the **lowest** result (instead of highest).

**Rationale:**
- Every starting character has one Approach at 0
- Preserves player agency (can attempt anything, just poorly)
- 75% Setback chance creates strong incentive to seek help without hard-locking players
- Simpler than conversion rules (6s→1s)
- Uses existing "take one die" mechanic, just inverted

**Probabilities:** 75% Setback, 22% Conflict, 3% Success, 0% Triumph
