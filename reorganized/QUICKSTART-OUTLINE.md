# Quick Start Guide Outline

**Purpose:** Get players from zero to first session in 10 pages  
**Audience:** New players and GMs who want to start playing immediately  
**Model:** Inspired by Ironsworn Chapter 1 (28 pages → condensed to 10)

## Page Breakdown

### Page 1: What Is This Game?

**Half-page introduction:**
- You're heroes on perilous quests in a dangerous fantasy world
- Fiction-first: describe what you do, roll when it's risky/difficult/dramatic
- Collaborative storytelling with dice-based resolution
- One GM (or play co-op/solo with modifications)

**What You Need:**
- 3-7 six-sided dice per player
- Character sheet (provided)
- Pencils and paper
- 2-4 hours for first session

**Visual:** Staged photo or illustration showing dice pool

### Pages 2-3: The Core Loop

**The Six-Step Sequence:**

1. **GM frames the situation** — "You're scaling the cliff face. Halfway up, rocks crumble..."
2. **Player declares action** — "I grab for a handhold and keep climbing" (Defy)
3. **GM assesses difficulty and stakes** — "That's Limited (you lack gear), Regular Stakes"
4. **Player rolls dice** — Approach + Boons - Banes (cap ±2d), take highest
5. **Everyone interprets outcome** — Setback/Conflict/Success/Triumph
6. **GM narrates result** — What happens, apply consequences if any

**Worked Example:**
Full play-through of one action from start to finish with:
- Fiction before mechanics
- Dice shown visually
- Outcome interpretation
- Consequence application
- Return to fiction

**Callout Box:** "This is the game. Everything else is detail."

### Page 4: Making Your Character

**Five Quick Steps:**

1. **Assign Approaches** (3/2/1/1/0)
   - Daring, Grace, Charm, Cunning, Spirit
   - What each represents in one sentence

2. **Write Three Traits**
   - Origin: Where you're from
   - Profession: What you do
   - Personal: Who you are
   - One example of each

3. **Choose Your Calling**
   - Brief description of all six
   - Note: Full details in main book

4. **Choose 2 Additional Talents**
   - From your Calling list
   - Note: Full list in main book

5. **Record Starting Stats**
   - Stress: 5 boxes
   - Momentum cap: 2
   - Rank: 1 (Local Heroes)

**Visual:** Blank character sheet with annotations

**Callout Box:** "Character creation takes 15-20 minutes. Full details in Chapter 7."

### Page 5: How to Roll Dice

**The Dice Pool:**
- Start with your Approach (0-3 dice)
- Add Boons (+dice)
- Remove Banes (-dice)
- Cap at ±2d after Boons/Banes cancel
- Minimum pool: 2d (take lowest if you have 1d or less)

**The Four Outcomes:**
- **Setback** (all 1-3): Failure + Major Consequence
- **Conflict** (any 4-5): Success + Minor Consequence  
- **Success** (any 6): Clean success
- **Triumph** (two+ 6s): Heroic success + Momentum

**Boon Sources:**
- Favorable positioning
- Proper tools
- Ally assistance
- Spend Momentum (Create an Opening)
- Invoke a Talent

**Bane Sources:**
- Poor positioning
- Lacking tools
- Complications (enemy abilities)
- Conditions (injuries, fear, etc.)

**Visual:** Dice pool example with math shown

**Expertise:** If you have Expertise in this domain, Setback → Conflict (you succeed, but at cost)

---

### Page 6: The Five Actions

What you can do when the dice come out.

**Defy** → Overcome obstacles, resist danger, dodge attacks
- On success: overcome simple obstacle OR mark 1 on Progress track

**Clash** → Force compliance through violence
- On success: mark Harm track (violence) or Will track (threats)

**Talk** → Change minds through persuasion
- On success: mark Will track

**Sense** → Ask questions, gather information
- On Conflict or Success: ask 2 questions (or 3 with Greater Effect)

**Know** → Recall knowledge
- On Conflict or Success: GM tells you one specific thing + you ask clarifying question

**When to Roll:**
- Difficult, Dangerous, or Dramatic
- Don't roll for trivial tasks or when failure isn't interesting

---

### Page 7: Consequences and Resilience

**Consequence Tiers:**
- **Minor:** Mark 1 Stress OR gain Tier 1 Condition
- **Major:** Mark 2 Stress OR gain Tier 2 Condition OR mark Progress track against you
- **Severe:** Mark 3 Stress OR gain Tier 3 Condition OR lasting setback
- **Disaster:** (rare) Taken Out or Burden

**Stakes modify tiers:** Low (-1 tier), Regular (no change), High (+1 tier)

**Stress Track:** 5 boxes
- Push Yourself: Mark 1 Stress for 1 Boon on your roll
- Assist ally: Mark 1 Stress for 1 Boon on their roll
- Overflow: Choose Burden or Taken Out

**Conditions:** Named effects (Wounded, Frightened, Exhausted)
- Tier 1-3, each applies 1 Bane
- Clear on Short Rest (1 hour, safe location)

**Burdens:** Persistent Conditions
- Require Long Rest or special narrative resolution

**Visual:** Stress track example with overflow choice

---

### Page 8: Momentum and Traits

**Momentum** (metacurrency, cap 2)

**Earn Momentum:**
- Roll Triumph (double 6s)
- Invoke Trait with Build Momentum effect
- Invoke Your Drive (your motivation creates a genuine complication)

**Spend Momentum:**
- **Create an Opening (1 Momentum):** Your action gains 1 Boon + 1 Edge (ignore 1 Complication)
- **Resist Consequence (1 Momentum):** Roll Defy to reduce/avoid any consequence

**Traits** (Origin, Profession, Personal)

When narratively relevant and you roll **Conflict or better**, invoke your Trait for a **Special Effect:**
- **Greater Effect:** Mark 2 on track instead of 1, OR ask bonus question
- **Secondary Effect:** Free Defy action (Take Something, Seize Control, Create Advantage, Change Situation)
- **Build Momentum:** Grant 1 Momentum to self or ally

**One Trait per roll**

**Visual:** Momentum flow (earn → spend)

### Page 9: For the GM

**GM Agendas:**
1. Portray a compelling world
2. Fill the characters' lives with adventure
3. Play to find out what happens

**Core GM Principles:**
- Be a fan of the characters
- Ask questions and build on the answers
- Telegraph danger before it strikes
- Failure is not a wall, it's a complication
- Think offscreen too

**Assessing Difficulty (Vantage):**
- **Certain:** No roll needed (automatic success)
- **Strong:** Standard roll (1 Boon if advantaged)
- **Limited:** Roll with 1-2 Banes (poor tools/positioning)
- **Impossible:** Cannot attempt (suggest different approach)

**Setting Stakes:**
- **Low Stakes:** Consequence tier -1 (learning, practice, low-threat)
- **Regular Stakes:** Standard consequences (most situations)
- **High Stakes:** Consequence tier +1 (deadly foes, desperate moments)

**Complications:** Enemy abilities that reduce outcome tiers
- Resilient (tough armor), Elusive (hard to hit), Resolute (strong will), etc.
- Players negate with Edge (Create an Opening, Triumph)

**GM Moves:** When outcome demands action:
- **Story Moves:** Reveal information, change environment
- **Suspense Moves:** Telegraph future danger (soft) or make danger imminent (hard)
- **Impact Moves:** Apply consequences from rolls

**First Session Tips:**
- Start with action (mid-scene, not tavern)
- Ask players to describe their characters in action
- Telegraph Vantage and Stakes before rolls
- Let failure create interesting complications
- Keep Momentum flowing (reward bold choices)

## Design Notes for Quick Start

### Constraints
- **10 pages maximum** (strict)
- **No prior RPG knowledge assumed**
- **Get to first roll by page 3**
- **Character creation on one page**
- **Reference viable** (not just read-once)

### What's Excluded
- Spellcasting (too complex for Quick Start)
- Detailed Talent lists (link to main book)
- Advancement (not needed for first session)
- Extended examples (one example only)
- Design rationale (not needed to play)
- Edge cases (main book handles these)
- Callings detail (brief descriptions only)

### What's Emphasized
- **The core loop** (pages 2-3, prominent)
- **Rolling dice** (page 5, visual)
- **Consequences** (page 7, immediately relevant)
- **GM guidance** (page 9, essential for first session)

### Visual Design Priorities
1. Dice pool examples (page 5)
2. Character sheet walkthrough (page 4)
3. Core loop flowchart (pages 2-3)
4. Stress track example (page 7)
5. Momentum flow (page 8)

### Callout Boxes
- "This is the game. Everything else is detail." (page 3)
- "Character creation takes 15-20 minutes" (page 4)
- Pool minimum rule (page 5)
- When to roll vs not (page 6)
- Stakes modify consequences (page 7)

### Cross-References
Every section that's condensed links to main book chapter:
- "Full details in Chapter X"
- "See Part 3 for complete reference"
- Clear signposting so readers know where to find more
