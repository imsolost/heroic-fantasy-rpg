# Chapter 7: Character Creation

This chapter walks you through building a hero step by step. It assumes you've read Part 1 (The Rules) and understand how Action Rolls, Traits, and Momentum work. If anything here is unfamiliar, the cross-references point you back to the relevant rules chapter.

**The seven steps:**
1. Concept
2. Approaches
3. Traits
4. Calling & Talents
5. Drive
6. Connections
7. Final Details

---

## Step 1: Concept

Who is your character? Define them in 1-2 sentences — enough to guide every decision that follows.

**Examples:**
- "A disgraced knight seeking redemption for a past failure"
- "A street urchin who discovered magical talents and fled the city"
- "A wandering scholar researching ancient ruins and forgotten gods"
- "A hunter exiled from her clan, tracking the beast that killed her sister"

Your concept doesn't need to be original — it needs to be evocative enough that you can picture how this person acts under pressure. The mechanics will make them unique.

---

## Step 2: Approaches

Assign the starting array **3 / 2 / 1 / 1 / 0** to your five Approaches:

| Approach | What It Represents |
|----------|-------------------|
| **Daring** | Bold, forceful, aggressive |
| **Grace** | Agile, precise, controlled |
| **Charm** | Warm, personable, likable |
| **Cunning** | Clever, indirect, calculating |
| **Spirit** | Willful, passionate, determined |

Your **3** is your primary method — how you solve most problems. Your **0** is a weakness or untrained area.

**See Chapter 2 for how Approaches interact with Actions and Vantage.**

### Choosing Your Array

Think about your concept and ask: *how does this character approach problems?*

| Concept | Likely Spread |
|---------|--------------|
| Berserker warrior | Daring 3, Spirit 2 |
| Nimble duelist | Grace 3, Cunning 2 |
| Charming trickster | Charm 3, Cunning 2 |
| Calculating strategist | Cunning 3, Spirit 2 |
| Zealous crusader | Spirit 3, Daring 2 |

There's no wrong assignment — your Approaches will grow through advancement (see Chapter 8).

---

## Step 3: Traits

Choose **3 Traits** — evocative narrative descriptors that define who your character is. When a Trait is relevant to your action, you gain a Special Effect (see Chapter 6).

**A useful framework:**

| Type | Question | Examples |
|------|----------|----------|
| **Origin** | Where are you from? | Cimmerian Barbarian, Fey-Blooded Bastard, Street Urchin of Ashport |
| **Profession** | What do you do? | Bladedancer, Ranger of the North, Demon Circus Acrobat |
| **Personal** | Who are you? | Unbreakable Survivor, Irrepressible Trickster, Seasoned Leader |

This framework isn't mandatory — use it as inspiration to create a well-rounded character.

### Writing Good Traits

**Be specific and evocative.** Add detail that grounds the Trait in your world:
- "Demon Circus Acrobat" instead of "Acrobat"
- "Shadow of the Thieves Guild" instead of "Thief"
- "Veteran of the Iron Wars" instead of "Soldier"
- "Raised by Wolves in the Ashen Wood" instead of "Raised by Wolves"

**Target: 30-50% of your rolls.** A Trait should apply frequently enough to feel like a core part of your identity, but not so broadly that it triggers on everything.

**Too broad:** "Competent" (applies to everything), "Lucky" (could justify any situation)

**Too narrow:** "Longsword Expert vs Knights" (one specific situation), "Knows Fire Elemental Weaknesses" (one-use knowledge)

**See Chapter 6 for complete Trait rules, Special Effects, and worked examples.**

### Example Characters

| Character | Origin | Profession | Personal |
|-----------|--------|------------|----------|
| Conan | Cimmerian Barbarian | Veteran Raider | Unbreakable Survivor |
| Indiana Jones | Fast-Talking Academic | Archaeologist | Daring Adventurer |
| Aragorn | Heir of Isildur | Ranger of the North | Seasoned Leader |
| Arys | Fey-Blooded Bastard | Glamour-Weaver | Irrepressible Trickster |

## Step 4: Calling & Talents

Choose a **Calling** — your hero's archetype and the source of their unique mechanical abilities.

### The Six Callings

| Calling | Identity | Core Fantasy |
|---------|----------|--------------|
| **Champion** | Martial master | Dominate through combat prowess |
| **Hunter** | Wilderness expert | Track, survive, strike from advantage |
| **Mystic** | Spiritual conduit | Channel divine or primal power |
| **Sage** | Arcane scholar | Reshape reality through knowledge |
| **Shadow** | Stealth specialist | Strike unseen, control information |
| **Vanguard** | Unbreakable protector | Shield allies, endure what others cannot |

Your Calling grants you a **Core Talent** — a defining ability that shapes how you interact with the game's core systems. Core Talents cannot be changed.

### Choose 1 Additional Talent

In addition to your Core Talent, choose **1 additional Talent** from your Calling's list. This is your starting specialization.

Talents are discrete mechanical abilities — they grant Expertise, unlock new moves, modify resource economies, or grant Edge in specific situations. They're the primary way characters differ mechanically.

**See Chapter 9 for complete Calling descriptions and Talent lists.**

## Step 5: Drive

Your **Drive** is what your character wants most — the motivation that pulls them forward into danger when staying home would be safer.

**Write one sentence:** What does your character pursue, protect, or long for?

**Optionally write one rejection:** What would they *never* pursue, even if offered freely?

**Example Drives:**

| Character | Drive | Rejection |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Disgraced knight | Redeem my name and prove my honor was real | I will never pursue power for its own sake |
| Street urchin mage | Find where my magic comes from | I won't seek belonging by conforming |
| Wandering scholar | Uncover what destroyed the old civilization | I will never prioritize safety over knowledge |
| Exiled hunter | Kill the beast that took my sister | I won't pursue vengeance against people — only monsters |

**Why Drive matters:**
- It tells the GM what temptations, dilemmas, and opportunities to put in front of you
- The rejection tells the GM what *won't* work as motivation — equally useful
- It pairs naturally with your Tangle (your Drive often creates the complications your Tangle represents)
- It gives the party a sense of what you'll fight for and what you'll refuse

**Keep it short.** One line each. Drive will evolve through play — what matters at creation is the starting vector, not the final answer.

## Step 6: Connections

**Connections** are relationships between player characters — established before play begins so the party has shared history from session 1.

### How to Build Connections

Go around the table. For each other PC, establish at least one connection using the prompts below or your own questions. Connections don't need to be reciprocal — your view of them may differ from their view of you.

**Ask another player one of these (or create your own):**

- What danger did I pull you out of, and what did it cost me?
- What secret have you told me that you haven't told the others?
- What do I do that annoys you, and why do you put up with it?
- What promise did we make to each other before this started?
- What did I see you do that made me decide to trust you?
- Why do you come to me when things go wrong?

**A player can decline any prompt** — it's fine if not every PC pair has a deep bond at the start. You can always discover connections through play.

### Connection Qualities

If it helps, note the *quality* of each connection:

| Quality | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| Trust | I believe what you say and rely on you |
| Debt | One of us owes the other something |
| Tension | We disagree about something important |
| Admiration | I respect something about you I lack |
| Protectiveness | I feel responsible for your safety |
| Rivalry | We compete, but not maliciously |

These aren't mechanical — they're shorthand for the table to understand party dynamics before play begins.

## Step 7: Final Details

Record the remaining information on your character sheet:

**Resilience:**

| Resource | Starting Value | Notes |
|----------|---------------|-------|
| **Stress** | 5 boxes | Your resilience buffer (see Chapter 4) |
| **Momentum Cap** | 2 | Maximum Momentum you can hold (see Chapter 5) |
| **Rank** | 1 (Local Heroes) | Your narrative scope (see Chapter 8) |

**Name** — Something that fits your world and feels right to say at the table.

**Appearance** — What do people notice first? 2-3 vivid details beat a full inventory.

**Tangle (optional)** — A personal complication that earns you Momentum when it creates trouble. Must be specific: "I owe the Thieves Guild a blood debt" not "I have a troubled past." See Chapter 5 for Tangle rules.

**Calling-Specific Questions (optional):** Your Calling in Chapter 9 includes 2-3 background questions that help you flesh out *why* you became this archetype and what you left behind. Answer them now or discover the answers through play.

## Character Creation Checklist

| Step | Decision | Record On Sheet |
|------|----------|-----------------|
| 1 | Concept (1-2 sentences) | — |
| 2 | Assign 3/2/1/1/0 to Approaches | Daring, Grace, Charm, Cunning, Spirit |
| 3 | Write 3 Traits | Origin, Profession, Personal |
| 4 | Choose Calling + 1 additional Talent | Calling, Core Talent, Additional Talent |
| 5 | Drive (+ optional rejection) | What you pursue / what you won't |
| 6 | Connections (at table, with other players) | At least 1 per other PC |
| 7 | Name, appearance, resilience, Tangle (opt.) | 5 Stress, 2 Momentum cap, Rank 1 |
