# Chapter 12: GM Moves

> *Name the move in your head, describe it in fiction.*

GM Moves are your toolkit for responding to the fiction. They give structure to your improvisation — when something happens, you pick the right type of move and deliver it through narration. Players never hear "I'm making a Suspense Move." They hear "The dragon's throat glows orange as flames gather in its maw..."

## The Three Tiers

### Story Moves (Always Available)

Story Moves are your default. Use them anytime to establish the world, frame situations, and create context for play. They cost nothing and require no trigger.

**Story Moves:**
- Describe the environment
- Introduce NPCs
- Frame scenes and set context
- Ask questions ("What does this place remind you of?")
- Show off-screen events ("Meanwhile, in the capital...")
- Establish mood and atmosphere

**Examples:**
- "The merchant eyes you suspiciously."
- "You hear distant thunder rolling across the moors."
- "The tavern is packed with nervous townsfolk whispering about the missing children."

**When:** Anytime. No trigger needed. No resource cost. This is you painting the world.

---

### Suspense Moves (Telegraph / Setup)

Suspense Moves escalate tension and telegraph danger. They're warnings — signals that something is about to happen, giving players the chance to respond before consequences land.

**Suspense Moves:**
- **Warn of imminent danger** — "The dragon's throat glows orange — flames are gathering!"
- **Show signs of approaching threat** — "You hear heavy footsteps echoing down the hall."
- **Put someone in a spot** — "The bridge is collapsing. Do you jump or grab your ally?"
- **Offer an opportunity, with or without cost** — "You could grab the artifact, but the guardian is turning..."
- **Reveal an unwelcome truth** — "You recognize the bandit leader — it's your brother."
- **Advance a countdown** — "The ritual is halfway complete. Dark energy swirls."

**When to use:**
- PC rolls Conflict (success + complication — use a Suspense Move for the complication)
- You want to create pressure or build to something bigger
- The fiction demands a warning before consequences
- You're spending Suspense to create dramatic escalation

### Two Types of Warning

**Imminent Danger** (immediate threat, demands response now):
- Enemy prepares to attack: "The ogre raises its club high!"
- Trap activates: "The pressure plate clicks beneath your foot!"
- Environment collapses: "The ceiling cracks — it's coming down!"
- Gives PCs an immediate opportunity to react (usually Defy)

**Approaching Threat** (longer-term escalation, creates time pressure):
- Off-screen danger: "You hear guards approaching."
- Countdown advances: "The ritual reaches its second phase."
- Situation worsens: "Smoke begins filling the room."
- Creates urgency — PCs need to wrap up or face new complications

---

### Impact Moves (Consequence / Follow-Through)

Impact Moves deliver consequences. They're the payoff of Suspense — danger that was telegraphed now strikes. Or they're the immediate result of a PC's failure.

**When to use:**
- PC rolls **Setback** → Major Consequence
- PC rolls **Conflict in combat** → Minor Consequence (enemy responds)
- A Suspense Move was **ignored or failed** → Consequence lands
- A **Countdown completes** → The promised danger arrives
- The **fiction demands immediate consequence** (player handed you the opportunity)

### Consequence Severity

**Minor Consequence (from Conflict):**
Mark 1 Stress, impose tier 1 Condition, lose position, attract attention, or introduce a complication.

**Major Consequence (from Setback):**
Mark 2 Stress, enemy counterattacks, lose something important, trigger telegraphed danger, or the objective fails.

**Severe Consequence (from Disaster or Countdown completion):**
Taken Out, Burden inflicted, objective fails catastrophically, or the situation changes dramatically against the PCs.

## The Hint → Reveal → Strike Pattern

The core pattern for telegraphing danger. Never ambush players without warning (unless surprise is narratively justified).

### 1. Hint (Subtle Clue)

Foreshadow danger without spelling it out. No roll required — just information.

- "The corridor ahead is oddly silent."
- "Scratch marks on the door, like claws."
- "The merchant's smile doesn't reach his eyes."

### 2. Reveal (Obvious Danger)

Make the threat clear and present. Players must respond.

- "The tripwire glints in your torchlight."
- "You hear growling from the darkness ahead."
- "The merchant's hand moves to his dagger."

### 3. Strike (Threat Acts)

Danger follows through. Deliver an Impact Move.

- "The trap triggers. Defy with Grace or mark 2 Stress."
- "The beast lunges from the shadows. What do you do?"
- "The merchant draws and stabs. Roll Defy!"

**Give players opportunity to respond at each step.** Only Strike if they ignore warnings or fail to stop the threat. Skip stages only when surprise is narratively justified (ambush by an invisible foe, betrayal they couldn't have seen coming).

## Suspense (GM Resource)

Suspense is a **visible** GM resource that tracks escalating tension. Players can see your Suspense pool — this makes danger transparent and escalation feel earned rather than arbitrary.

### Starting Suspense

- **Combat or dangerous scene:** Start with Suspense = number of PCs (typically 2-5)
- **Exploration or social:** Start at 0, build as tension rises

### Earning Suspense

- **PC rolls Setback:** +1 Suspense
- **PCs trigger danger:** +1 Suspense (spring trap, alert guards, break truce)
- **Time passes or situation worsens:** +1 Suspense (GM discretion, use sparingly)

### Spending Suspense

- **Suspense Move (0-1 Suspense):** Most Suspense Moves are free. Spend 1 for especially dramatic escalation.
- **Impact Move (1-2 Suspense):** Deliver consequence outside of a PC's roll result. Enemy acts, danger strikes, environment shifts.
- **Activate special ability (1-3 Suspense):** Boss uses signature Challenge Move, reinforcements arrive, battlefield changes.
- **Seize Initiative (1-2 Suspense):** Enemy acts immediately, interrupting the current spotlight.

### Why Suspense is Visible

- Players understand threat level: "The GM has 4 Suspense — this is serious."
- Spending feels earned: "You rolled Setback, I gain 1 Suspense and spend it to trigger the trap."
- Creates dramatic rhythm: tension builds → spends → builds again.

**Don't hoard Suspense.** Spend it to create drama, pressure, and memorable moments. An empty Suspense pool is the calm before the storm.

## The Consequence Menu

When a roll produces a consequence, choose from this menu based on what fits the fiction. Consequences should always make the fiction more interesting and open new problems.

### The Character Is Hurt

- An enemy attacks them (mark Stress)
- They suffer a Condition (Wounded, Frightened, Stunned)
- They're knocked down, cornered, or exposed

### Their Standing Is Harmed

- They offend someone important
- They break the law or violate a norm
- They lose an ally's trust
- They gain unwanted attention

### They Lose Something

- An item is stolen, lost, or broken
- A costly demand is made
- Resources are depleted (torch, ration, rope)
- Information is compromised

### The Environment Shifts

- Terrain becomes hostile (fire spreads, floor collapses)
- Escape routes close
- A disaster strikes or advances
- The situation becomes more complex

### Their Enemies Advance

- A villain gains power or completes a step
- Allies lose resources or position
- Danger spreads to innocents
- A Countdown track advances

### Tough Choices (Especially Good for Conflict)

Offer two equally undesirable consequences — the player chooses one:

- Deal reduced effect OR mark Stress
- Take a Condition OR lose/break an item
- Save one ally BUT not another
- Accomplish the goal BUT alert guards
- Retreat to safety OR let innocents suffer

Tough choices turn Conflict from "success minus a bit" into dramatic decisions. The player chooses *which* consequence, not *whether* there's a consequence.

## The Decision Matrix

| Situation | Move Type | Why |
|-----------|-----------|-----|
| PC rolls Conflict in combat | Impact Move (Minor) | Enemy responds to PC's action |
| PC rolls Setback | Impact Move (Major) | They failed — consequence lands |
| Players ask "What do we see?" | Suspense Move | Telegraph danger, create choice |
| Ignoring warnings / time pressure | Impact Move (Major) | Fiction demands escalation |
| Enemy's turn in combat | Impact Move | Use Challenge Moves if available |
| Countdown completes | Impact Move (Severe) | Deliver promised consequence |
| PC rolls Success or Triumph | No move needed | Clean victory, no complication |

## How Moves Flow at the Table

### Typical Combat Round

1. PC acts (rolls Clash to attack)
2. Resolve outcome + any Complications
3. If Conflict or Setback → GM makes Impact Move (enemy responds)
4. Next player in spotlight
5. Repeat

### Typical Exploration Scene

1. Players describe what they do
2. If outcome uncertain → call for a roll
3. If Conflict → Suspense Move (telegraph danger)
4. Players respond to danger
5. If they ignore or fail → Impact Move (danger strikes)

### Countdown Pattern

1. Suspense Move: Describe initial threat ("The ritual has begun.")
2. Mark Countdown track (openly or secretly)
3. Suspense Move: Escalate each tick ("The ritual is halfway complete — the air crackles.")
4. Impact Move when Countdown completes: Deliver promised consequence

## Spotlight Management

### "What Do You Do?"

This is your key question. After presenting a situation — danger, opportunity, complication — ask this to prompt player response. Then wait. Let them think. Let them talk to each other.

**Good framing:**
- "The captain charges at you, sword raised. What do you do?"
- "The ritual circle flares — you have seconds. What do you do?"
- "The merchant offers you the poisoned cup with a smile. What do you do?"

**Poor framing:**
- "Roll Defy." (prescribing the action — let them choose)
- "You get hit for 2 Stress." (no agency — where's their chance to respond?)
- "What do you do?" (without context or stakes — what are they responding to?)

### Rotating the Spotlight

**Default:** Ask individual PCs based on the fiction:
- Who's in danger?
- Who just acted and triggered a response?
- Whose turn feels right dramatically?

**When uncertain:** Go clockwise around the table.

**When urgent:** Interrupt with enemy action (spend Suspense), then ask "What do you do?"

**Seize Spotlight (1 Momentum):** A player can spend 1 Momentum to act immediately, interrupting the current spotlight. This is their normal turn — it doesn't grant an additional action later.
