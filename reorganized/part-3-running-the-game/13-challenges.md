# Chapter 13: Challenges

> *A good Challenge isn't a wall to break through — it's a puzzle that rewards creative thinking.*

Challenges are adversaries, obstacles, and situations that oppose the PCs. This chapter teaches you how to design them — from Complication tags and progress tracks to boss encounters and group combat.

## What Are Challenges?

Challenges are anything that opposes the PCs:

- **Enemies:** Knights, dragons, assassins, bandits
- **Obstacles:** Locked doors, cliffs, traps, riddles
- **Social opposition:** Suspicious nobles, hostile factions, stubborn officials
- **Environmental:** Blizzards, collapsing bridges, raging rivers

**Not all Challenges need special mechanics.** Simple opposition just uses Vantage to determine difficulty. A locked door might just be a single roll. A random wolf might just be narrated away.

**Significant Challenges** — the ones that create extended engagement — have **Complications** (passive defenses) and **Challenge Moves** (active abilities).

---

## Rank and Challenge Rating

These are two independent scales that measure different things.

### Rank (Narrative Scope)

Rank measures narrative significance and typical Complication count:

| Rank | Scope | Typical Complications |
|------|-------|----------------------|
| 1 | Local stakes | 0 (1 rarely) |
| 2 | Regional stakes | 0-1 (2 rarely) |
| 3 | National stakes | 1-2 (3 rarely) |
| 4 | World-ending stakes | 1-3 (4 rarely) |

### Challenge Rating / CR (Durability)

CR measures how long it takes to overcome — the track size:

| CR | Track Length | Typical Use |
|----|-------------|-------------|
| 1 | 2 ticks | Quick encounters, minions |
| 2 | 4 ticks | Standard encounters, balanced fights |
| 3 | 6 ticks | Boss fights, major obstacles |
| 4+ | 8+ ticks | Epic encounters, legendary entities |

### Why They Don't Match

**Rank = defensive complexity. CR = fight duration.** They serve different purposes and combine freely.

**CR 4, Rank 1 — The Goblin King:**
Long fight (8 ticks), but 0-1 Complications. Climactic finale for starting heroes — tough but not defensively complex.

**CR 1, Rank 3 — Demon Spawn Minions:**
Quick to defeat (2 ticks), but 1-2 Complications each. Legendary heroes cutting through individually dangerous but fragile hordes.

**CR 2, Rank 4 — Demigod Soldier:**
Standard durability (4 ticks), but 1-3 Complications. Intensely dangerous but can be defeated in a single focused confrontation.

## Progress Tracks

Challenges can have one or more progress tracks. Track selection is a **design decision** that tells players what approaches will work.

### Harm Track

Represents physical damage and bodily defeat. When a Harm track fills, the adversary is defeated, incapacitated, or dead.

**Marked by:** Clash (physical attacks), Defy when causing damage (shoving off a cliff, tripping into fire), environmental harm.

| CR | Track Length | Examples |
|----|-------------|----------|
| 1 | 2 ticks | Goblins, bandits — one solid hit defeats them |
| 2 | 4 ticks | Knights, veterans — requires sustained effort |
| 3 | 6 ticks | Dragons, war trolls — extended combat |
| Boss | 8-10 ticks | Legendary adversaries, climactic encounters |

### Will Track

Represents mental and emotional resistance — composure, determination, resolve. When a Will track fills, their resistance collapses: they flee, surrender, concede, or break down.

**Marked by:** Talk (persuasion, deception, influence), Clash with social delivery (intimidation, taunts, psychological pressure).

| Strength | Track Length | Examples |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| Weak-willed | 2 ticks | Frightened civilians, demoralized soldiers |
| Average | 4 ticks | Trained guards, minor nobles |
| Strong-willed | 6 ticks | Military commanders, fanatical cultists |
| Unbreakable | 8+ ticks | Legendary figures, iron-willed villains |

**Talk vs Clash for Will:** Both mark Will, but the outcome differs. Talk = convinced (positive relationship possible). Clash = defeated socially (coerced, fearful, relationship damaged).

### Obstacle Track

Represents barriers, puzzles, wards, and inanimate challenges that must be bypassed or solved.

**Marked by:** Defy (climbing, lockpicking, disabling traps, sneaking past, solving puzzles, dispelling wards).

| Complexity | Track Length | Examples |
|------------|-------------|----------|
| Simple | 2 ticks | Basic lock, climbable wall |
| Standard | 4 ticks | Complex mechanism, warded door |
| Complex | 6 ticks | Ancient vault, elaborate trap network |
| Monumental | 8-10 ticks | Legendary seal, epic-scale obstacle |

### Track Selection as Design Tool

Track selection signals what approaches will work:

| Tracks | Signal | Example |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Harm only | Must be destroyed physically | Golem, zombie |
| Will only | Cannot be solved with violence | Political rival, stubborn official |
| Obstacle only | Inanimate, requires skill | Locked door, trapped hallway |
| Harm + Will | Fight or persuade | Bandit, knight, dragon |
| Harm + Will + Obstacle | Multiple valid solutions | Dragon (fight, negotiate, or sneak past) |

**Defeating through any track ends the encounter.** You don't need to fill both. An adversary with Harm 4 / Will 4 can be defeated by filling either — the approach determines the fictional outcome.

### Asymmetric Tracks

Track lengths can differ to create tactical puzzles:

- **War Troll** (Harm 6, Will 10): Physically powerful, effectively immune to persuasion — you must fight.
- **Zealot Priest** (Harm 2, Will 6): Physically frail but fanatically resolved — violence is easy but may create martyrdom.
- **Cowardly Thug** (Harm 4, Will 2): Tough but easily scared — intimidation is faster than combat.

## Complication Tags

Complications reduce your outcome by **-1 tier** after rolling. Each uncanceled Complication shifts your result one step down (Success → Conflict → Setback → Disaster). See **Chapter 3: Consequences** for the complete mechanical framework.

**Triumph bypasses all Complications entirely.** It cannot be downgraded.

### The Eight Tags

Complications are tagged to indicate what they hinder. Only relevant tags apply — check each action against the fiction.

---

**Resilient** — Hard to damage or break.
- Hinders actions that harm, damage, or destroy
- Armor, tough hide, reinforced construction, magical durability
- Countered by: overwhelming force, finding weak points, armor-piercing attacks

**Warded** — Resists magical effects.
- Hinders non-damaging magical effects and manipulations
- Magic immunity, counterspells, ancient enchantments
- Does NOT apply to damaging spells (those face Resilient)
- Countered by: dispelling, overwhelming magical power, different approach

**Elusive** — Hard to affect, detect, or track.
- Hinders attempts to target, locate, or pursue
- Supernatural speed, small size, invisibility, flight, phasing
- Countered by: area effects, revealing techniques, restraints, prediction

**Vigilant** — Watchful and aware.
- Hinders actions relying on stealth, deception, or surprise
- Keen senses, paranoia, trained observation, tremorsense
- Countered by: distraction, overwhelming senses, magical concealment

**Complex** — Hard to understand, solve, or navigate.
- Hinders actions requiring comprehension of intricate systems
- Complex mechanisms, codes, layered defenses, political webs
- Countered by: knowledge, time, expertise, simplifying the problem

**Resolute** — Hard to influence or sway.
- Hinders social or mental influence (mundane or magical)
- Discipline, faith, stubbornness, strong convictions
- Countered by: appealing to values, leverage, building trust

**Legendary** — Always applies.
- Hinders ALL actions regardless of approach
- Overwhelming mythic presence, reality-warping nature
- Countered by: Triumph, Create an Opening, exploiting weaknesses

**Deadly** — Applies when attacking or being attacked.
- Hinders combat actions (offense and defense)
- Combat mastery, superior weapons, size advantage, pack tactics
- Does NOT apply to non-combat actions (negotiation, investigation)
- Countered by: Triumph, Create an Opening, avoiding direct confrontation

---

### Only Relevant Tags Apply

**This is critical.** A Challenge may have three Complication tags, but only those matching your specific action apply.

**Example — The Vault Door (Complex + Warded + Resilient):**

- **Smash it down (Clash/Daring):** Only Resilient applies (1 Complication)
- **Pick the lock (Defy/Cunning):** Only Complex applies (1 Complication)
- **Dispel the ward (Know/Spirit):** Only Warded applies (1 Complication)

Three tags doesn't mean "impossible." It means "choose your approach carefully."

**Example — Ancient Dragon (Resilient + Warded + Vigilant + Legendary):**

- **Sword strike:** Resilient + Legendary = 2 Complications
- **Charm spell:** Warded + Legendary = 2 Complications
- **Intimidation:** Only Legendary = 1 Complication (social is the weak point!)

### Bypassing Complications

**1. Face Them (Standard):** Roll normally, Complications reduce outcome. Spend Momentum for Edge (Create an Opening) or use Talents that grant Edge.

**2. Bypass Temporarily (Setup Action):** One character creates advantage for an ally. A Secondary Effect (Create Advantage) grants the ally 1 Edge — their next action ignores 1 Complication.

- Sense a weak point in armor → ally bypasses Resilient
- Cast Light → ally bypasses Elusive against the revealed foe
- Pin the shield arm → ally bypasses Resilient for one attack

**3. Remove Permanently (Fictional Elimination):** Change the fiction so the Complication source no longer exists.

- Disarm the Deadly sword → remove Deadly until they retrieve it
- Destroy the ward stone → remove Warded permanently
- Shove enemy from fortified position → remove positional Complication

## Challenge Moves

Challenge Moves are powerful active abilities that Challenges use as Impact Moves. They represent signature attacks, special abilities, and dramatic actions — more potent than standard consequences.

### Power Level

- Standard Impact Move: 2 Stress
- Challenge Move: 3 Stress, OR 2 Stress + Condition, OR area effects, OR special abilities

This makes telegraphs meaningful — players *want* to avoid Challenge Moves.

### Format

`Move Name [□□]` — Checkboxes show limited uses per encounter.

### Power Guidelines

**CR 1-2 (Standard foes):**
- 2 Stress single target
- OR 1-2 Stress + Condition tier 1-2
- Simple, straightforward effects

**CR 3-4 (Elite/Boss):**
- 3 Stress single target (hard cap)
- OR 2 Stress + Condition tier 2-3
- OR Area effects (multiple targets, 1-2 Stress each)
- OR Environmental Complications

### Limited Uses

Some Challenge Moves have limited uses to represent exhaustion or cooldowns.

**When to limit:** Powerful effects, signature abilities, exhausting actions (breath weapons, magical bursts).

**Default:** Most Challenge Moves are unlimited unless noted.

### Environmental Complications

Some Challenge Moves create **temporary Environmental Complications** — battlefield effects with a duration track:

**Format:** `Complication Name (Tag) [□□□]`

- Created by Challenge Moves
- Small track (2-3 boxes)
- Tick down as PCs take affected actions
- Expire when track empties or at end of scene

**Examples:**
- **Smoke Cloud (Elusive) [□□□]** — Next 3 Sense/ranged actions suffer +1 Complication
- **Wall of Thorns (Complex) [□□]** — Next 2 movement attempts suffer +1 Complication
- **Aura of Fear (Resolute) [□□□]** — Next 3 social actions in zone suffer +1 Complication

## Boss Design

Boss encounters should require **team coordination** and **multiple approaches**.

### Elements of a Boss

**High Rank + Multiple Complications:**
- Rank 3-4 baseline
- 2-3 Complications
- At least one exploitable weakness

**Longer Track:**
- 8-10 ticks instead of 4-6
- Creates multi-round engagement

**Multiple Challenge Moves:**
- 2-4 signature abilities
- Mix unlimited basics with limited specials
- At least one area effect

**Environmental Factors:**
- Hazardous terrain (Banes or Environmental Complications)
- Dynamic battlefield (objectives beyond "kill the boss")
- Lair actions tied to Suspense spending

### Multi-Phase Design

At 50% of the track, the boss changes tactics:

- Dragon becomes enraged and takes flight
- Lich's phylactery activates new defenses
- Knight discards damaged armor for speed

Telegraph the phase shift: "Something changes in its eyes..."

### Example Boss: Ancient Lich

```
Ancient Lich (CR 4, Rank 4)
Tracks: Harm 10, Will 8, Obstacle 6
  - Harm: Physical destruction (very durable undead body)
  - Will: Breaking its grip on this plane (holy/divine persuasion)
  - Obstacle: Finding and destroying phylactery (hidden, warded)

Complications:
  - Resilient — Undead resilience (holy/divine attacks negate)
  - Warded — Ancient arcane protections
  - Complex — Lair defenses (applies to bypassing traps/obstacles)

Exploitable: Phylactery (destroying via Obstacle track removes Resilient)

Challenge Moves:
  - Deathly Aura (Passive) — All PCs mark 1 Stress at start of each round
  - Raise Dead [□□□] — Summon 2-tick minion in Near range (3 uses)
  - Soul Drain [□□] — Target marks 2 Stress + Exhausted 2 (2 uses)
```

**How PCs defeat it:**

1. **Direct combat (Harm 10):** Long, brutal. Requires holy weapons to bypass Resilient + teamwork to overcome Warded.
2. **Banishment ritual (Will 8):** Divine/holy ritual to sever its connection to this plane.
3. **Destroy phylactery (Obstacle 6):** Find it, bypass wards, destroy it → removes Resilient entirely.
4. **Mixed approach (optimal):** Split efforts — some distract the Lich while others find the phylactery. Once destroyed, coordinated assault finishes it.

Multiple tracks create strategic depth. Players must choose which victory condition to pursue, or split resources.

## Group Tracking

A single track can represent multiple enemies acting as a coordinated group. This simplifies bookkeeping for mass combat while keeping the fiction dynamic.

### How It Works

Instead of tracking 6 individual goblins, use **one track representing the squad**. When you mark ticks, describe individual enemies falling. The track measures the group's remaining threat.

- Success → 1 tick (one goblin drops)
- Greater Effect → 2 ticks (two goblins drop)
- Triumph + Greater Effect → 3+ ticks (you cut through several)

When the track fills, the group is defeated, scattered, or routed.

### Tier Scaling for Groups

Grouping increases effective tier by +1:

- Single goblin (no track) → Goblin squad of 3-6 (CR 1, 2 ticks)
- Single soldier (CR 1, 2 ticks) → Soldier squad of 4-6 (CR 2, 4 ticks)
- Single veteran (CR 2, 4 ticks) → Veteran warband (CR 3, 6 ticks)

Exact headcount is flexible — "a squad" might be 4 or 8. The track measures collective threat, not precise numbers.

### Group Complications

Groups can have Complications representing collective advantages:

- Armored knights in formation → **Resilient**
- Pack of wolves → **Deadly** (coordinated attacks)
- Stealthy assassin cell → **Elusive**

### Single Group vs Multiple Enemies

**Model as single Challenge when:**
- The group acts as a coordinated unit
- You want simplified tracking
- Their coordination is the source of danger (use Deadly)

**Model as multiple separate Challenges when:**
- Each enemy is significant or unique
- Enemies act independently with different tactics
- You want spotlight on individual opponents

**Hybrid approach:**
- **Bandit Squad** (single 4-tick, Deadly) + **Bandit Captain** (separate 4-tick, Resilient)
- **Wolf Pack** (single 4-tick, Deadly) + **Alpha Wolf** (separate 6-tick)

Gives you fodder plus a spotlight enemy without overwhelming bookkeeping.

## Running Multi-Challenge Scenes

A single scene can contain multiple Challenges and tracks running simultaneously:

- **Multiple Challenges:** 3 goblins (CR 1 group) + orc captain (CR 2, Resolute)
- **Challenge + Countdown:** Lich (Harm 10) + Countdown "Phylactery ritual completes" (8 ticks)
- **Multiple Tracks:** Progress "Navigate the labyrinth" (6 ticks) + Countdown "Minotaur finds you" (4 ticks)
- **Layered Threats:** Boss fight (Harm track) + environmental hazard (Countdown) + hostages in danger

Each Challenge and track operates independently. Mark them based on fiction:
- PC attacks → mark Harm track
- Time passes or PCs fail → mark Countdown track
- Successful navigation → mark Progress track
- Environmental triggers → use as Suspense/Impact Moves

## Consequence Calibration

New GMs often struggle with consequence severity. Use these templates for consistency.

### Minor Consequence (Conflict)

| Situation | Options |
|-----------|---------|
| Physical combat | 1 Stress, OR Condition tier 1, OR lose position |
| Social conflict | 1 Stress, OR relationship damaged, OR caught in a lie |
| Exploration | 1 Stress, OR time passes, OR alert raised |

### Major Consequence (Setback)

| Situation | Options |
|-----------|---------|
| Physical combat | 2 Stress, OR 1 Stress + Condition tier 2-3, OR weapon breaks |
| Social conflict | 2 Stress, OR relationship broken, OR objective fails |
| Exploration | 2 Stress, OR fall/trap triggers, OR resource lost |

### Severe Consequence (Disaster)

Any situation: Taken Out, Burden inflicted, objective fails catastrophically, or Countdown completes.

### The Golden Rule

**Telegraph before striking.** If players saw the danger coming and had a chance to respond, the consequence feels earned. If it blindsides them, it feels arbitrary — even if mechanically identical.

## Telegraphing Multiple Complications

When a Challenge has 2+ Complications, its dominance must be obvious in the fiction. Multiple Complications signal that direct action is extremely dangerous.

**Always state Complications upfront:**

- "The knight's plate armor is impenetrable, and his shield guard leaves no openings. That's two Complications if you attack directly."
- "The dragon's scales shimmer with magical wards. Two Complications — you'll need to find a weakness or coordinate an assault."

This signals to players:
1. Frontal assault will likely fail
2. They should use Sense to find weaknesses
3. They should Build Momentum for coordinated strikes
4. They should use Create an Opening to gain Edge
5. Alternate approaches may bypass defenses entirely

## Challenge Design Quick Guide

### Design Steps

1. **Pick CR** based on desired fight length (2 = standard, 3 = boss, 4+ = epic)
2. **Pick Rank** based on narrative scope (determines Complication budget)
3. **Assign tracks** based on valid approaches (Harm, Will, Obstacle)
4. **Choose Complications** based on Rank guidelines (tag them appropriately)
5. **Design Challenge Moves** if significant (2-3 for bosses)
6. **Add exploitable trait** (weakness players can discover and leverage)

### By Complication Count

- **0 Complications:** Standard opposition, routine encounters
- **1 Complication:** Competent to dangerous, requires setup or Momentum
- **2 Complications:** Elite, requires teamwork or exceptional rolls
- **3+ Complications:** Boss-tier, requires coordination + Triumph/Create an Opening/weaknesses. Use sparingly (2-3 times per campaign arc).

## Complete Design Example: Drow Assassin

**Concept:** Elite killer hired to eliminate the party's patron. Dangerous in combat, hard to pin down, favors hit-and-run tactics.

**Step 1 — CR:** Significant boss-tier but beatable with coordination. **CR 2** (4-tick Harm).

**Step 2 — Rank:** Legendary-tier threat requiring tactical thinking. **Rank 3** (allows 2-4 Complications).

**Step 3 — Tracks:** Can be fought physically (Harm) or negotiated with (Will). **Harm 4, Will 6** (disciplined but not immune to a better offer).

**Step 4 — Complications:** Elite combatant with poisoned weapons (Deadly) + uses shadows and speed (Elusive). **Total: 2.**

**Step 5 — Challenge Moves:**
- Poisoned Blade — Close range, 1 Stress + Poisoned 3 (unlimited)
- Shadow Step [□□] — Teleport to Near/Far position (2 uses)
- Smoke Bomb [□] — Create Smoke Cloud (Elusive) [□□□] (1 use)

**Step 6 — Exploitable:** "Professional" — Can be bought off. Appeals to self-interest grant 1 Boon on Talk rolls.

```
Drow Assassin (CR 2, Rank 3)
Tracks: Harm 4, Will 6
Complications: Deadly, Elusive
Exploitable: "Professional" (contract appeals grant 1 Boon on Will rolls)
Challenge Moves:
  - Poisoned Blade — Close, 1 Stress + Poisoned 3 (unlimited)
  - Shadow Step [□□] — Teleport to Near/Far (2 uses)
  - Smoke Bomb [□] — Create Smoke Cloud (Elusive) [□□□] (1 use)
```

**How this plays:** Direct combat faces 2 Complications (hard). Social approach faces long Will track but no Complications (offer a better contract). PCs need teamwork for combat OR creative negotiation for social victory.
