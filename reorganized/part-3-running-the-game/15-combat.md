# Chapter 15: Combat

> *Turn order emerges from fiction and dramatic pacing, not rigid counting.*

This chapter adds tactical depth to combat — spotlight rotation, zones, maneuvers, and epic foe abilities. The core combat mechanics live in Part 1 (Actions, Consequences, Stress). This chapter is about *running* combat well.

## Turn Structure

There is **no formal initiative system**. The GM follows the spotlight based on fiction — who's in danger, who just acted, whose turn feels dramatically right.

**Default rotation:** Ask individual PCs based on the fiction:
- Who's in immediate danger?
- Who just triggered a response?
- Whose turn feels right dramatically?

**When uncertain:** Go clockwise around the table.

**When urgent:** Spend Suspense to Seize Initiative — an enemy acts immediately, interrupting the spotlight.

### Player Option: Seize Spotlight

**Cost:** 1 Momentum

A player can act immediately, interrupting the current spotlight. This is their normal turn — it doesn't grant an extra action later.

**Example:** Kael sees the orc warboss winding up for a devastating attack on Mira. He spends 1 Momentum to Seize Spotlight and intercepts the blow.

**Timing:** Declare at any time when another PC has the spotlight or when an enemy is about to act (after a Suspense Move but before an Impact Move resolves).

### GM Option: Seize Initiative

**Cost:** 1-2 Suspense

An enemy acts immediately, interrupting the current spotlight. Use this to create urgency and make combat feel dynamic.

## Zones and Positioning

Combat areas can be divided into abstract **zones** for tactical clarity while preserving theater-of-mind flexibility.

### Zone Ranges

- **Close:** Same zone, melee range (engaged combatants)
- **Near:** Adjacent zone, short move or ranged attack
- **Far:** 2+ zones away, long move or distant ranged attack

### Movement

**Moving as part of an action:** When you make an action roll (Clash, Defy, Talk), you can move to a Near zone as part of that action — describe it in your approach.

**Moving without another action:** If you want to reposition (Near or Far) without doing anything else, Defy to move safely under pressure.

**Moving through enemy zones:** Always requires a Defy roll — enemies try to intercept or block.

### Positioning Benefits

The GM grants Boons or imposes Banes based on fictional positioning:

**Advantageous (Boon):**
- High ground
- Cover from ranged attacks
- Flanking an enemy with an ally
- Superior terrain (stable vs. slippery)

**Disadvantageous (Bane):**
- Surrounded
- Pinned down or cornered
- Poor footing (narrow ledge, debris, ice)
- Exposed to multiple threats

### Ranged Attacks

- **Close/Near:** No penalty with clear line of sight
- **Far:** 1 Complication unless weapon has "Long Range"

### When to Use Zones

Use zones when tactical positioning matters — castle siege, multi-level dungeon, environmental hazards. For simple skirmishes, narrative positioning ("you're pressed against the wall") is sufficient.

**Example Zones:**
- **Tavern brawl:** Bar area, main floor, kitchen, street outside
- **Forest ambush:** Trail, treeline left, treeline right, dense forest
- **Castle courtyard:** Gatehouse, courtyard center, ramparts

## Combat Maneuvers

Maneuvers are just **regular Actions with declared intent**. You don't need special mechanics — describe what you're trying to do, the GM determines the Action and difficulty, you roll.

### The Principle

- **Success or higher:** You achieve your intent
- **Conflict or higher + Trait:** Achieve intent AND may invoke Trait for Special Effect
- **Setback:** Fail and suffer consequence

### Maneuvers Are Often Defy

Many maneuvers map directly to Defy's established outcomes:

- **Take Something:** Disarm, steal, seize position
- **Seize Control:** Knockdown, pin, push, grapple, force into corner
- **Create Advantage:** Expose weakness, blind with sand, distract
- **Change Situation:** Kick brazier, trigger trap, create cover

### Special Effects Add Extra

On Conflict or better, a relevant Trait can add a Special Effect on top of your primary intent:

- Clash to mark Harm + Secondary Effect: Seize Control (hit AND knock them down)
- Defy to Seize Control + Secondary Effect: Take Something (shove aside AND steal their dagger)

### Example Maneuvers

**Disarm** — "I hook his sword with my axe and twist."
- Clash/Daring (if dealing damage too) OR Defy/Grace (Take Something)
- Success: Enemy drops weapon

**Knockdown** — "I sweep his legs as he overextends."
- Clash/Grace (damage) OR Defy/Grace (Seize Control)
- Success: Enemy knocked prone, vulnerable to follow-up

**Shove** — "I shield-bash him toward the cliff edge."
- Clash/Daring (damage) OR Defy/Daring (Seize Control)
- Success: Move enemy one zone (into hazard, away from ally)

**Create Opening** — "I feint high to draw his guard up."
- Clash/Cunning (damage) OR Defy/Cunning (Create Advantage) OR Sense (spot weakness)
- Success: Ally gains 1 Boon on next action against target

### GM Guidance

**Fictional positioning matters.** Disarming requires melee range. Shoving off a ledge requires a ledge. Blinding with sand requires sand. Fiction first, always.

**Don't gate maneuvers behind Talents** unless they're extraordinary (disarming at range, sundering legendary equipment, instant kills without setup).

## Working Together in Combat

### Assist (Mark 1 Stress)

Grant an ally 1 Boon on their roll. Describe how you're helping: "I shout a warning!" or "I steady your aim!"

Doesn't consume your action. Multiple allies can Assist the same roll (each marking 1 Stress), but the +2 Boon cap still applies.

### Push Yourself (Mark 1 Stress)

Gain 1 Boon on your own roll. Declare before rolling.

### Setup (Your Action)

Use your action to create an opening for an ally. GM grants them 1 Boon on their next related action at no cost to them.

### Build Momentum (Trait Special Effect)

When you invoke a Trait for Build Momentum, grant 1 Momentum to yourself or an ally. They can spend it to Create an Opening (1 Boon + 1 Edge) or Resist Consequence.

## Suspense in Combat

Suspense is a visible GM resource that creates dynamic enemy action. See **Chapter 12** for complete Suspense rules. Here's how it works specifically in combat.

### Starting Suspense

Begin combat with Suspense equal to number of PCs (typically 2-5).

### Earning Suspense in Combat

- PC rolls Setback → +1 Suspense
- PCs trigger danger → +1 Suspense (spring trap, alert reinforcements)

### Spending Suspense in Combat

- **Seize Initiative (1-2 Suspense):** Enemy acts immediately, interrupting spotlight
- **Activate Special Ability (1-3 Suspense):** Trigger Challenge Move (area attack, summon reinforcements, powerful effect)
- **Escalate (1 Suspense):** Environment shifts (fire spreads, ceiling collapses, storm intensifies)

### Example

> **GM:** "Kael, you roll Setback on your Clash. The orc captain laughs as your blade bounces off his shield. I gain 1 Suspense. I'm spending it to Seize Initiative — the captain bellows and two more orcs charge from the treeline. Mira, they're heading straight for you. What do you do?"

### Why It Works

- Visible pool makes escalation transparent
- Players understand threat level ("GM has 4 Suspense — this is serious")
- Spending feels earned, not arbitrary
- Creates dramatic rhythm: tension builds → spends → builds again

## Epic Foe Abilities

Epic Foes — bosses and major threats — have special abilities that create tactical depth. Give them **2-3 abilities maximum**. More overwhelms.

### Frightening Presence

When PCs first engage, each rolls Defy with Spirit:
- Setback → Frightened 2
- Conflict → Frightened 1
- Success/Triumph → Unaffected

**Example:** Dragon, demon lord, ancient vampire.

### Legendary Actions

GM spends 1-2 Suspense between PC actions to trigger an extra enemy action. Represents exceptional speed and power.

- Dragon tail sweep (1 Suspense)
- Kraken tentacle grab (1 Suspense)
- Lich counterspell (2 Suspense)

Use sparingly: 1-2 per round maximum.

### Area Attack

Affects all PCs in a zone. GM describes the attack; each PC rolls Defy to avoid or reduce consequences.

- Dragon breath (Defy/Grace to dodge)
- Giant's ground slam (Defy/Daring to brace)
- Necromancer's death wave (Defy/Spirit to resist)

### Multi-Phase

At 50% track, the foe changes tactics:
- Becomes enraged (remove a Complication, add Deadly)
- Takes flight (new zone, new Complication)
- Reveals true form (new Challenge Moves)
- Discards damaged armor for speed

Telegraph the shift: "Something changes in the dragon's eyes as half its scales are torn away..."

### Regeneration

Recover ticks at end of each round (or when specific condition is met). PCs must prevent it — fire stops troll regeneration, holy water stops vampire healing, destroying the phylactery stops the lich.

### Death Move

When defeated, the foe triggers one final action:
- Lich whispers a curse → PC gains Cursed 2 (Burden)
- Dragon's dying breath ignites the forest
- Warlord's final strike → mark 2 Stress if in Close range

## Pacing Combat

### By Enemy Type

**Minions (CR 1, 2 ticks):** Fast, disposable. Use in groups of 3-6. Describe defeats cinematically: "You cut through the goblins in a blur of steel."

**Standard Foes (CR 2, 4 ticks):** Competent opposition. Small groups of 2-4. Each gets a moment to act and react.

**Elite Warriors (CR 3, 6 ticks):** Dangerous individuals. Solo or pairs. Describe injuries accumulating: "He's bleeding but still dangerous..."

**Epic Foes / Bosses (CR 4+, 8-10 ticks):** Centerpiece encounters. Give special abilities, multiple phases, and Challenge Moves. These fights should take 15-30 minutes.

### General Pacing Tips

- **Open with action.** Start combat with an enemy charging, an explosion, or a dramatic reveal — not "roll initiative."
- **End quickly when decided.** Once the outcome is clear (boss at 1 tick, PCs overwhelming), narrate the conclusion rather than rolling out every last hit.
- **Vary encounter length.** If last fight was 20 minutes, make the next one 5 minutes. If players just had a brutal boss, give them a quick minion cleanup to feel powerful.
- **Use environment.** Every fight should have at least one Feature or Threat players can interact with. Empty rooms are boring rooms.

### Tracking Enemy Debuffs

When players place debuffs or Conditions on enemies, allies gain 1 Boon on actions against that target.

**Duration:**
- Normal spell/Secondary Effect: 1-2 ally actions (GM decides)
- Potent Spell with duration: All ally actions this scene

**Tracking:** Write the debuff and remaining count near the enemy token. Tick down after each ally action that uses the advantage.

## Extended Combat Example

**Setup:** Kael (warrior), Mira (ranger), Ryn (rogue) face an **Orc Warboss** (8 ticks, Resilient + Deadly) and **3 Orc Minions** (2 ticks each, grouped as CR 2).

**Round 1:**
- **Kael** Defies the Warboss's charge → Conflict → Mark 1 Stress, pressed back
- **Mira** Clashes lead orc → Success → Squad track: 2 ticks (one drops)
- **Ryn** uses Sense on the Warboss → Triumph → Spots exposed shoulder, gains Momentum, asks questions about weaknesses

**Round 2:**
- **Kael** spends Momentum to Seize Spotlight, exploits Ryn's discovery → Clash → Success → 2 ticks on Warboss (weakness bypasses Resilient)
- **Warboss** counterattacks (Impact Move) → Kael Defies with Mira's Assist (she marks 1 Stress for Boon) → Success → Clean dodge
- **Ryn** backstabs Warboss → Success → 2 ticks (4/8 total)

**Round 3:**
- Remaining orcs engage Mira → She Defies → Conflict → Mark 1 Stress
- **Mira** Clashes orc → Triumph → Remaining orcs drop, gains Momentum
- **Kael** attacks Warboss → Success → 2 ticks (6/8)

**Round 4:**
- **Kael** finishing blow → Conflict → 2 ticks (8/8, defeated) + Mark 1 Stress from dying strike
- **Death Move:** Warboss curses Kael → Defy → Conflict → Cursed 1

**End result:** Kael 4/5 Stress + Cursed 1, Mira 2/5 Stress, Ryn unscathed.

**Key tactical choices:** Ryn used Sense to bypass Complications. Kael spent Momentum to Seize Spotlight at the right moment. Mira Assisted to protect Kael. Triumph bypassed the orcs' remaining defenses.
