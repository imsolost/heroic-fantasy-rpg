# ARCHON
## Quick Start Guide

**Get from zero to your first session in 10 pages**

*A heroic fantasy roleplaying game of perilous quests and bold action*

---

# Page 1: What Is This Game?

In this tabletop roleplaying game, you and your friends become heroes on perilous quests in a dangerous fantasy world. You'll explore ancient ruins, battle fearsome monsters, negotiate with powerful nobles, and uncover dark secrets—all through collaborative storytelling guided by dice.

**This is a fiction-first game.** You describe what your character does, and when the outcome is uncertain, risky, or dramatically significant, you roll dice to see what happens. The dice don't tell you what actions you can take—the fiction does. If you can describe it and it makes sense in the story, you can attempt it.

**One player is the Game Master (GM)**, who portrays the world, the people in it, and the dangers the heroes face. Everyone else plays a hero, making choices that drive the story forward. The GM is a fan of the heroes—not an adversary.

**Collaborative storytelling with stakes.** Everyone contributes to the unfolding story. The dice add uncertainty, risk, and drama. Success is never guaranteed, but failure is never boring.

---

### What You Need

- **3-7 six-sided dice** per player (d6)
- **Character sheets** (provided in the main rulebook)
- **Pencils and paper** for notes
- **2-4 hours** for your first session

That's it. No miniatures, maps, or elaborate prep required. Just imagination, dice, and friends.

---

### What Makes This Game Different?

**Five clear actions.** Instead of a long list of skills, you have five broad actions that cover everything: Defy, Clash, Talk, Sense, and Know. What you're trying to accomplish is clear from the action you choose.

**Fiction-first resolution.** You roll dice when what you're attempting is difficult, dangerous, or dramatic—not because a rule told you to roll.

**Momentum and setbacks.** Heroic successes earn Momentum, which you spend to create openings or resist consequences. The game flows between triumph and adversity.

**Traits over skills.** Instead of numerical skills, you have Traits—short phrases describing who you are and where you come from. When relevant, they give you powerful options.

**Dice pools, not modifiers.** You roll multiple dice and take the highest result. More dice = better odds, and it's visually satisfying to roll a handful of dice.

# Pages 2-3: The Core Loop

**This is the game. Everything else is detail.**

Every significant moment in the game follows this six-step sequence:

---

## 1. GM Frames the Situation

The GM describes what's happening in the world. They set the scene, introduce problems, and portray the people and dangers the heroes encounter.

> **GM:** "You're halfway up the cliff face when the rocks beneath your hands crumble. Loose stones cascade down into the ravine below. The cliff's edge is still twenty feet above you, and your handholds are giving way. What do you do?"

---

## 2. Player Declares Action

You describe what your character does—what they're attempting and how. Choose one of the five actions based on what you're trying to accomplish:

- **Defy** — Overcome an obstacle, resist danger
- **Clash** — Force compliance through violence
- **Talk** — Change someone's mind
- **Sense** — Gather information, ask questions
- **Know** — Recall something you'd reasonably know

> **Player:** "I grab for a more secure handhold and keep climbing. I'm using all my strength to haul myself up the remaining distance." *(This is Defy—overcoming an obstacle)*

---

## 3. GM Assesses Vantage

The GM evaluates **Vantage**—your capability, tools, and circumstances:

- **Certain** → Automatic success (no roll needed)
- **Strong** → Roll with 1-2 Boons (advantageous position)
- **Standard** → Roll with no modifier (baseline)
- **Limited** → Roll with 1-2 Banes (disadvantaged position)
- **Impossible** → Can't attempt (suggest different approach)

**Optional: High Stakes**  
For rare climactic moments (boss fights, life-or-death), the GM may declare **High Stakes**—consequences are one tier more severe (Conflict becomes Major, Setback becomes Severe).

> **GM:** "This is **Limited Vantage**—the cliff is crumbling and you lack proper climbing gear. That's 1 Bane. On Conflict, you make it but suffer strain. On Setback, you fall and take serious harm."

The GM announces Vantage (and High Stakes if applicable) **before you roll**, so you know what you're getting into. You can always choose a different approach if you don't like the odds.

---

## 4. Player Rolls Dice

Build your dice pool:
- Start with your **Approach** (0-3 dice based on your character)
- Add any **Boons** (+dice from favorable circumstances)
- Remove any **Banes** (-dice from obstacles)
- **Minimum pool:** If your pool is 0 or less, roll 2 dice and take the **lowest** (instead of highest)

Roll your dice and look at the **highest single die** (or lowest if pool was 0):

> **Player:** "My Daring is 2, so I start with 2 dice. The GM gave me 1 Bane for the crumbling cliff, so I remove 1 die. That leaves me with 1 die."
> 
> *Rolls: 5 → Result is 5*

---

## 5. Everyone Interprets the Outcome

Look at your highest die to determine the outcome:

- **Setback** (all 1-3) — Failure with a **Major Consequence**
- **Conflict** (any 4-5) — Success with a **Minor Consequence**
- **Success** (any 6) — Clean success
- **Triumph** (two or more 6s) — Heroic success + gain Momentum

> **Result:** Highest die is **3** → **Setback**

---

## 6. GM Narrates the Result

The GM narrates what happens based on the outcome, applying any consequences.

> **GM:** "You lunge for a handhold, but the rock face crumbles under your weight. You fall—fifteen feet down into the ravine. You land hard on loose scree. Mark 2 Stress from the impact."

The consequence reflects the outcome (Setback = Major Consequence). The fiction drives what happens next.

---

### Complete Example

Let's see the full sequence in action:

**Setup:** The party is sneaking through a bandit camp at night. The Champion wants to silently take out a guard.

1. **GM frames:** "You creep toward the guard's back. He's alert, scanning the treeline. Firelight flickers across the camp."

2. **Player declares:** "I close the distance and strike him in the back of the head with my pommel, trying to knock him out quietly." *(Defy—overcoming the obstacle of the guard)*

3. **GM assesses:** "You're a trained warrior and the guard is distracted—that's **Strong Vantage** (1 Boon). But if you botch this, his shout will wake the whole camp—I'm declaring **High Stakes** (consequences are more severe if you fail)."

4. **Player rolls:** Daring 3 + 1 Boon (Strong Vantage) = 4 dice. *Rolls: 2, 4, 6, 6 → Highest is 6*

5. **Outcome:** **Success** (rolled a 6)

6. **GM narrates:** "You move like a shadow. Your pommel strike connects perfectly—the guard crumples without a sound. You lower him to the ground and drag him into the darkness. The camp sleeps on."

**That's the game.** Every risky action follows this loop: frame → declare → assess → roll → interpret → narrate. Everything else in this guide adds detail to these six steps.

# Page 4: Making Your Character

Character creation takes 15-20 minutes. You make five quick decisions, and you're ready to play.

---

## Step 1: Assign Your Approaches (3/2/1/1/0)

**Approaches** represent how you act, not what you do. You have five Approaches, and you assign these values: **3, 2, 1, 1, 0**.

- **Daring** — Forceful, aggressive, courageous action
- **Grace** — Precise, swift, controlled action
- **Charm** — Empathic, magnetic, persuasive action
- **Cunning** — Clever, deceptive, analytical action
- **Spirit** — Focused, tenacious, willful action

Your Approach determines how many dice you roll. Higher Approaches = more dice = better odds.

**Example:** A warrior might have Daring 3, Grace 2, Spirit 1, Cunning 1, Charm 0.

---

## Step 2: Write Your Three Traits

**Traits** are short phrases describing who you are. You have three:

- **Origin** — Where you're from or what shaped you
- **Profession** — What you do or trained to do
- **Personal** — A defining personality trait or belief

**Examples:**
- Origin: *Raised by Wolves*, *Street Urchin from the Capital*, *Exiled Noble*
- Profession: *Mercenary Veteran*, *Wandering Herbalist*, *Former Assassin*
- Personal: *Never Leaves a Friend Behind*, *Suspicious of Authority*, *Seeking Redemption*

When a Trait is relevant to what you're doing, you can invoke it for powerful benefits (see page 8).

---

## Step 3: Choose Your Calling

Your **Calling** is your heroic identity—what sets you apart. Choose one:

- **Champion** — Peak martial skill and indomitable will
- **Shadow** — Master of stealth, infiltration, and cunning
- **Warden** — Guardian of the natural world and its creatures
- **Invoker** — Channels raw magical power through Spheres of magic
- **Chronicler** — Scholar, diplomat, and master of knowledge
- **Blade** — Swift, precise warrior who dances through combat

Each Calling gives you a **Core Talent** (unique ability) and access to a list of **Additional Talents** (choose 2 now).

*(Full Calling details in Chapter 9 of the main rulebook)*

---

## Step 4: Choose 2 Additional Talents

From your Calling's Talent list, choose 2. Talents give you special abilities, Expertise in specific areas, or options to Push Yourself (mark Stress to gain an advantage).

**Example Champion Talents:**
- **Battleborn** — Gain Edge in battle; mark to Push Yourself or Resist without cost
- **Iron Will** — Expertise against fear, coercion, and mental control
- **One Against Many** — Gain Edge when alone against multiple foes

*(Full Talent lists in Chapter 9 of the main rulebook)*

---

## Step 5: Record Starting Stats

- **Stress Track:** 5 boxes (mark when you suffer harm or push yourself)
- **Momentum Cap:** 2 (increases to 3 with certain Talents)
- **Rank:** 1 (Local Heroes—you're skilled, but not legendary yet)

**You're ready to play!**

# Page 5: How to Roll Dice

When you make an action roll, you're building a **dice pool** and looking for the **highest single die**.

---

## Building Your Dice Pool

**Start with your Approach** (0-3 dice)
- Your Approach score = starting dice

**Add Boons** (+dice)
- Favorable positioning or circumstances
- Proper tools or preparation
- Ally assistance
- Spending Momentum (Create an Opening)
- Invoking a Talent

**Remove Banes** (-dice)
- Poor positioning or circumstances
- Lacking proper tools
- Complications (enemy abilities)
- Conditions (injuries, fear, exhaustion)

**Boons and Banes cancel 1:1**, then cap at ±2 dice remaining.

**Minimum pool:** If your pool is 0 or less, roll 2 dice and take the **lowest** result (instead of highest). This represents a desperate, clumsy attempt when you lack capability or favorable circumstances.

---

## Example Dice Pool

You're attacking an armored knight with your sword.

- **Daring 3** → Start with 3 dice
- **Proper weapon** → Already accounted for (no bonus)
- **Enemy has Resilient (tough armor)** → Complication (reduces outcome tier after roll)
- **Ally is flanking with you** → +1 die (1 Boon from positioning)

3 dice + 1 Boon = **4 dice total**

*Roll 4d6: 2, 4, 5, 6 → Highest is **6** = **Success***

But the enemy's Resilient Complication reduces the outcome: Success → Conflict

**Final result:** Conflict (you hit, but mark 1 Stress from the strain)

---

## The Four Outcomes

Look at your **highest single die**:

**Triumph** (two or more dice show 6)
- Heroic success
- Gain 1 Momentum + ignore all Complications

**Success** (at least one die shows 6)
- Clean success
- No consequences

**Conflict** (at least one die shows 4-5)
- Success with **Minor Consequence**
- You accomplish your goal, but there's a cost

**Setback** (all dice show 1-3)
- Failure with **Major Consequence**
- The GM makes a hard move against you

---

## Special Rule: Expertise

If you have **Expertise** in what you're doing (granted by Talents or your Calling), **Setback becomes Conflict**. You succeed, but at a cost.

**Example:** A Champion with weapon Expertise rolls all 1-3s against an armored foe. Expertise converts the Setback to Conflict—the attack lands (mark Harm track) but costs Stress.

Expertise represents mastery—you don't fail, but difficult situations still extract a price.

---

## When Do You Roll?

Roll when an action is:
- **Difficult** — Requires significant skill or effort
- **Dangerous** — Involves risk of harm or consequences
- **Dramatic** — A pivotal moment that affects the story

**Don't roll for:**
- Trivial tasks your character can obviously do
- Situations where failure wouldn't be interesting
- Actions the GM wants to say "yes" to automatically

---

# Page 6: The Five Actions

Every roll uses one of five actions. The action determines **what you're trying to accomplish**.

---

## Defy → Overcome

**Use Defy when you want to overcome an obstacle, resist danger, or avoid harm.**

- Dodge an attack
- Leap across a chasm
- Resist poison or fear
- Pick a lock
- Climb a wall
- Endure harsh weather

**On Success:** Overcome simple obstacle automatically OR mark 1 on a Progress track (for complex obstacles like "escape the collapsing ruin").

---

## Clash → Harm

**Use Clash when you want to force compliance through violence or the threat of violence.**

- Attack with a weapon
- Grapple or wrestle
- Threaten someone into submission
- Break down a door with force

**On Success:** Mark the target's **Harm track** (physical violence) or **Will track** (threats and intimidation).

*Clash breaks resistance. It's about overpowering, not persuading.*

---

## Talk → Influence

**Use Talk when you want to change someone's mind through persuasion, negotiation, or deception.**

- Convince a guard to let you pass
- Negotiate a deal with a merchant
- Deceive an enemy about your intentions
- Comfort a frightened ally
- Inspire troops before battle

**On Success:** Mark the target's **Will track** (if they're resisting) OR they agree to what you're asking (if they're neutral or friendly).

*Talk changes minds. It's about persuasion, not threats.*

---

## Sense → Assess

**Use Sense when you want to gather information by observing, investigating, or interacting with your environment.**

**On Conflict or Success:** Ask the GM **2 questions** (or 3 with Greater Effect from invoking a Trait). The GM answers honestly.

**Useful questions:**
- What here is useful or valuable?
- What recently happened here?
- What's about to happen?
- What here is not what it seems?
- Who or what is the greatest threat?
- How can I [accomplish specific goal]?

---

## Know → Recall

**Use Know when you want to recall information your character would reasonably know.**

- Recall legends about this ancient ruin
- Remember a noble's political allegiances
- Identify an herb or poison
- Recognize a monster's weaknesses

**On Conflict or Success:** The GM tells you **one specific, actionable thing** your character knows. You may ask **one clarifying question**.

*The GM decides what your character would know based on their Traits, Calling, and background.*

---

## Clash vs Talk: When to Use Which?

- **Threatening** someone to comply = **Clash** (mark Will through intimidation)
- **Persuading** someone to comply = **Talk** (mark Will through diplomacy)
- **Attacking** someone = **Clash** (mark Harm through violence)

---

# Page 7: Consequences and Resilience

When you roll Setback or Conflict, you suffer **consequences**.

---

## Consequence Tiers

**Minor** (from Conflict)
- Mark 1 Stress
- OR gain a Tier 1 Condition
- OR minor narrative setback

**Major** (from Setback)
- Mark 2 Stress
- OR gain a Tier 2 Condition
- OR mark a Progress track against you (lose ground)

**Severe** (from Disaster—very rare)
- Mark 3 Stress
- OR gain a Tier 3 Condition
- OR lasting narrative setback (disarmed, captured, betrayed)

**Disaster** (Setback reduced further by Complications)
- Taken Out OR permanent Burden

---

## High Stakes (Optional)

For rare climactic moments, the GM may declare **High Stakes** before you roll:
- Conflict → **Major** Consequence (instead of Minor)
- Setback → **Severe** Consequence (instead of Major)

**Use High Stakes for:** Boss fights, life-or-death moments, catastrophic potential failures

**Most rolls don't need High Stakes**—the default consequence tiers handle most situations.

---

## Your Stress Track (5 Boxes)

**Mark Stress when you:**
- Suffer physical harm
- Push yourself beyond limits
- Endure exhausting or stressful situations

**Use Stress to:**
- **Push Yourself:** Mark 1 Stress to gain 1 Boon on **your own roll**
- **Assist an ally:** Mark 1 Stress to give 1 Boon to **their roll**

**When your Stress fills completely:**
- You can't mark more Stress
- If forced to, choose: take a **Burden** (persistent Condition) OR be **Taken Out** (removed from the scene)

---

## Conditions

**Conditions** are temporary effects like Wounded, Frightened, Exhausted, Blinded, or Poisoned.

- Each Condition has a **tier** (1-3)
- Each Condition applies **1 Bane** to relevant actions (not cumulative per Condition)
- **Tick down** Condition tiers when you act against them (fight through the pain, resist the fear)
- **Clear completely** on a Short Rest (1 hour in a safe location)

**Example:** You're **Wounded (Tier 2)** and **Frightened (Tier 1)**. Physical actions have 1 Bane from Wounded, and actions toward the fear source have 1 Bane from Frightened—but you only have 2 Banes total, not 3.

---

## Burdens

**Burdens** are persistent Conditions that don't clear on Short Rest.

- Require a Long Rest (8 hours of safety and comfort) OR special narrative resolution
- Examples: *Cursed by Fae*, *Haunted by Betrayal*, *Permanent Injury*

---

## Taken Out

When Taken Out, you're removed from the scene (unconscious, captured, fled in terror, etc.). You're not dead unless the fiction demands it. You recover after the immediate danger passes.

---

## Rests

**Short Rest** (1 hour, safe location)
- Clear all Conditions (but not Burdens)
- Don't clear Stress

**Long Rest** (8 hours, safe and comfortable)
- Clear all Stress
- Clear all Conditions (including Burdens)

---

# Page 8: Momentum and Traits

---

## Momentum (Cap: 2)

**Momentum** is the game's metacurrency. You earn it through heroic successes and spend it to create opportunities or resist harm.

---

### Earn Momentum

**Roll Triumph** (two or more 6s)
- Gain 1 Momentum immediately

**Invoke a Trait with "Build Momentum"** (see Traits below)
- Grant 1 Momentum to yourself or an ally

**Trigger a Tangle** (optional rule)
- Introduce a character-driven complication (your personality, desires, or backstory creates trouble)
- Gain 1 Momentum

---

### Spend Momentum

**Create an Opening (1 Momentum)**
- Your action gains **1 Boon** (+1 die)
- AND **1 Edge** (ignore 1 Complication)
- Declare before rolling

**Resist Consequence (1 Momentum)**
- After a consequence is announced, spend 1 Momentum to roll **Defy**
- On Success: reduce consequence by 1 tier OR avoid it entirely
- On Conflict: reduce consequence by 1 tier
- On Setback: consequence happens as stated

---

## Traits (Origin, Profession, Personal)

When a Trait is **narratively relevant** to your action and you roll **Conflict or better**, you may invoke your Trait (after rolling) to gain a **Special Effect**:

---

### Greater Effect

**Mark 2 on a Progress track instead of 1** (Harm, Will, Obstacle)
- OR **Ask 1 additional question** (when using Sense)

**Example:** Your Origin *Raised by Wolves* is relevant when tracking prey through the forest. You roll Conflict while using Sense. Invoke your Trait to ask 3 questions instead of 2.

---

### Secondary Effect

**Gain a free Defy action** related to your initial action. Choose one:
- **Take Something** from the scene or target
- **Seize Control** of the situation (change who has advantage)
- **Create Advantage** (grant yourself or an ally 1 Boon for the scene)
- **Change Situation** (alter the environment or circumstances)

**Example:** Your Profession *Mercenary Veteran* is relevant when battling bandits. You roll Success on Clash (mark Harm). Invoke your Trait for Secondary Effect: *Seize Control* of the fight by forcing them into a choke point.

---

### Build Momentum

**Grant 1 Momentum** to yourself or an ally.

**Example:** Your Personal Trait *Never Leaves a Friend Behind* is relevant when you drag a wounded ally to safety under fire. You roll Success. Invoke your Trait to grant your ally 1 Momentum for their heroism.

---

**One Trait per roll.** You can only invoke one Trait per action, even if multiple Traits are relevant.

# Page 9: For the GM

Running this game is about **portraying a compelling world**, **making the heroes' lives dramatic**, and **playing to find out what happens**.

---

## GM Agendas

These are your goals as GM:

1. **Portray a compelling world** — Make the world feel real, dangerous, and alive
2. **Fill the characters' lives with adventure** — Put obstacles, dangers, and opportunities in their path
3. **Play to find out what happens** — Be curious about outcomes; don't predetermine them

---

## Core GM Principles

- **Be a fan of the characters** — You want to see them succeed, struggle, and grow
- **Ask questions and build on the answers** — The players contribute to the world
- **Telegraph danger before it strikes** — "The dragon's maw glows orange" before it breathes fire
- **Think offscreen too** — The world doesn't stop when the camera is elsewhere
- **Failure is not a wall** — Failed rolls create complications, not dead ends
- **Honor the fiction** — If it makes sense in the story, it happens

---

## Assessing Vantage

**Vantage** represents the character's capability, tools, and circumstances.

**Certain** (no roll needed)
- Clear capability + proper tools + trivial task
- *Example: Champion swinging sword in combat, Shadow picking a simple lock with proper tools*

**Strong** (roll with 1 Boon)
- Advantageous position, ideal circumstances
- *Example: High ground + flanking, surprise attack, perfect setup*

**Standard** (roll with no modifier)
- Baseline—capable + proper tools, neutral conditions
- *Example: Climbing with rope and handholds, persuading a neutral guard*

**Limited** (1-2 Banes)
- Lacking ideal tools OR poor positioning
- *Example: Climbing without gear (1 Bane), climbing crumbling cliff without gear (2 Banes)*

**Impossible** (cannot attempt)
- Fundamentally incapable OR fiction doesn't support it
- *Example: Casting spells without being a spellcaster, attacking someone in another city*

**Announce Vantage before the player rolls** so they can adjust their approach or spend Momentum.

---

## High Stakes (Optional)

For **rare climactic moments**, the GM may declare **High Stakes**:
- Consequence tier +1 (Conflict → Major, Setback → Severe)
- *Example: Boss fight, defusing a bomb, life-or-death confrontation*

**Most rolls don't need High Stakes.** Vantage already handles difficulty; High Stakes is only for when failure demands exceptional weight.

**Announce High Stakes before the player rolls** so they understand the risk.

---

## Complications: Enemy Abilities

**Complications** are defensive abilities that reduce outcome tiers (Success → Conflict → Setback → Disaster).

Common Complication tags:
- **Resilient** — Tough armor or thick hide (hinders attacks)
- **Elusive** — Hard to hit (invisibility, speed, small size)
- **Resolute** — Strong-willed (hinders persuasion/intimidation)
- **Vigilant** — Keen senses (hinders stealth/deception)
- **Complex** — Intricate defenses (hinders attempts to solve/unlock)
- **Deadly** — Dangerous foe (hinders all combat actions)

Players negate Complications with **Edge** (from Create an Opening or Triumph).

**Announce Complications before players roll** so they know what they're up against.

---

## GM Moves: What to Do

When the dice demand it or the story calls for it, make a **move**:

**Story Moves** — Reveal information, introduce NPCs, change the environment

**Suspense Moves** — Telegraph danger (soft move) or make danger imminent (hard move)
- **Hint** → first warning
- **Reveal** → danger is clear
- **Strike** → danger hits (usually after Setback)

**Impact Moves** — Apply consequences from Setback or Conflict
- Mark Stress or Conditions
- Progress tracks against heroes
- Narrative setbacks

---

## First Session Tips

**Start with action** — Mid-scene, not in a tavern
- "You're already climbing the tower when the guards spot you."

**Ask players to describe their characters in action**
- "How do you scale the wall differently than your ally?"

**Telegraph Vantage and Stakes before rolls**
- "You lack climbing gear—that's 1 Bane. If you fall, it's a twenty-foot drop—High Stakes."

**Let failure create complications, not dead ends**
- Failed to pick the lock? The guard hears you and approaches.
- Failed to persuade the noble? They're now suspicious of your motives.

**Keep Momentum flowing**
- Reward bold choices with Boons
- Let players describe their Triumphs
- Encourage Trait invocations

**When in doubt, ask questions**
- "What's your character thinking right now?"
- "How does your Trait influence your approach?"
- "What do you do next?"
