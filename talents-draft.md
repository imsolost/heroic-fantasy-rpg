## New Talents (Adapted from Grimwild/Chasing Adventure)

### Risk/Reward Mechanics

**All or Nothing**
*Risk everything for glory.*

When you declare an action with **High Stakes**, you may invoke this Talent before rolling. If you do, treat all 5s as 6s on this roll. However, all 4s count as 3s (Conflict becomes Setback).

**Example:** "I leap the chasm to reach the collapsing bridge—all or nothing!"

---

**Driven By [Vice]**
*Your obsession grants strength, but demands sacrifice.*

**Character Creation:** Define your drive (glory, vengeance, wealth, knowledge, justice, etc.).

**When you refuse a golden opportunity** to pursue your drive despite obvious temptation, mark **1 Stress**.

**When you Push Yourself** on an action directly pursuing your drive, gain **+2 Boons** instead of +1 Boon (still mark 1 Stress).

**Example:** A glory-driven warrior refuses to chase fleeing enemies to save civilians (mark Stress). Later, she Pushes Herself to challenge the enemy champion in single combat (gain +2 Boons).

---

**Unstoppable**
*Pain only makes you stronger.*

When you would mark **Stress from a consequence**, you may instead take a **Condition tier 1** and choose one immediate benefit:
- Ignore being knocked prone or moved
- Make an immediate Clash counterattack (no roll, mark 1 tick)
- Move to any Near zone freely

**You still mark Stress as normal** if you already have that Condition active.

---

**Frenzy**
*You enter a battle-rage, heedless of pain.*

You have a **Frenzy tracker** (☐☐☐, replenishes on Short Rest).

**Entering Frenzy:** Mark 1 box as a free action during combat. While in Frenzy (lasts for the scene):
- **Ignore the first Condition tier 1** you would gain each round
- **Gain +1 Boon** on all Clash rolls
- **Mark +1 Stress** whenever you mark Stress from any source

**Exiting Frenzy:** You can't voluntarily exit until the scene ends or all enemies are defeated.

**Narrative:** Describe your frenzied state—rage, battle-joy, bloodlust, divine fury, etc.

---

### Defensive/Supportive

**Got Your Back**
*You never abandon an ally in danger.*

When an ally within Close range rolls **Setback**, you may immediately mark **1 Stress** to let them re-roll their Action. If their re-roll is Success or better, you both gain **1 Momentum**.

**Timing:** Declare after seeing the Setback result, before the GM narrates consequences.

---

**Guardian**
*You shield the innocent with your own body.*

When you **Assist an ally on a Defy roll** to avoid harm (dodge, resist, endure), you may mark **2 Stress instead of 1**.

If you do and they still roll **Setback**, you may **take the consequence yourself** instead of them (mark Stress, take Conditions, or suffer the narrative consequence). Describe how you intercept or shield them.

---

**Swift Recovery**
*Your resilience is legendary.*

When you roll **Triumph on a Defy roll** and currently have **2 or less Stress marked**, you may clear **1 Stress**. Describe how you recover quickly (shake it off, catch your breath, steel your nerves).

**Design Note:** Rewards defensive excellence when you're not already heavily stressed.

---

### Tactical/Positioning

**Hold the Line**
*None shall pass.*

**Once per scene**, you may declare a zone or chokepoint that enemies **cannot cross without engaging you**.

When an enemy attempts to move through or past your held position, you may immediately make a **Clash or Defy roll to intercept them**. On Success or better, they're stopped (describe how—you block, tackle, or threaten them). On Conflict, they're stopped but you mark 1 Stress. On Setback, they get through.

---

**Tactician**
*You read the battlefield like a book.*

When you roll **Success or better on a Sense roll** in combat or a tense situation, you may identify **up to 3 environmental features or tactical elements** (cover, high ground, hazards, enemy positions, escape routes, etc.).

**Allies who use these features** you identified gain **1 Boon on related actions this scene**.

**Example:** You spot: crumbling balcony above, oil barrels near the brazier, narrow doorway the ogre can't fit through. Allies gain Boon when using these.

---

**Challenge**
*Face me, coward!*

**Once per combat**, you may issue a formal challenge to a foe (they must be able to hear and understand you). Name them and declare your intent to face them.

**Benefits:**
- You gain **1 Boon on all rolls** directly against the challenged foe.
- The GM gains **+1 Suspense** immediately.

**Restriction:** If you take any action that doesn't directly involve confronting the challenged foe (helping allies, fleeing, attacking others), you lose this benefit.

**Design Note:** The Suspense cost represents the dramatic weight of your challenge—the GM can use it to make the duel more dynamic.

---

### Knowledge/Investigation

**Storied Knowledge**
*Your travels have taught you many tales.*

When you first encounter a significant creature, location, or artifact, you may declare **one thing you've heard about it** (a strength, weakness, history, or connection). The GM confirms it's true.

**The GM then adds one complication** you didn't know about.

**Example:** "I've heard the Crimson Duke is immune to mortal steel" (true). GM: "Yes, but you didn't know he can sense anyone who speaks his name within his domain."

---

**According to Plan**
*You prepared for this exact situation.*

**Once per session**, when you face a problem, you may declare **"I prepared for this"** and describe a flashback to your earlier preparation (set a trap, planted evidence, hid a weapon, bribed a guard, etc.).

Roll **Defy with Cunning**.

**Success:** Your preparation works as intended.
**Triumph:** It works better than expected—gain **1 Edge** on the next related roll.
**Conflict:** It works, but there's a complication (GM describes).
**Setback:** Your preparation has a critical flaw the GM will reveal at the worst moment.

---

### Social/Reputation

**Friendly Face**
*You make friends wherever you go.*

When you **arrive in a new settlement**, you may declare you have a **contact here**—someone you've met before or helped in the past.

Roll **Talk with Charm**:

**Triumph:** They owe you a significant favor and will help with dangerous or costly tasks.
**Success:** They're friendly and will help with small favors, information, or shelter.
**Conflict:** They'll help, but it'll cost you (coin, favor, or time).
**Setback:** They're present but unhappy to see you (you owe them, or they blame you for something).

**Name them and describe your history.**

---

**Fearsome Reputation**
*Your legend precedes you.*

**Define your reputation** (brutal conqueror, undefeated duelist, monster slayer, etc.). When intelligent creatures who might know your reputation encounter you, the GM should telegraph their reaction (fear, respect, hostility, awe).

**Mechanical Benefit:** When you face a **group of minions (3+ standard enemies)**, they do not gain Complications from their numbers. Your reputation makes them hesitate.

---

### Offensive/Damage Enhancement

**The Great Destroyer**
*You shatter weapons and armor like glass.*

When you roll **Triumph on a Clash roll**, you may invoke your Trait for **Secondary Effect: Take Something**. If you do, you **destroy or break one piece of their equipment** (weapon, shield, armor piece).

**If this would remove a Complication** (like "Armored" from breaking their shield), it does.

Describe the devastating blow that shatters their gear.

## Talents On Hold (Future Consideration)

### Champion
**Living Legend**
Your name and deeds precede you. When you reveal your identity or invoke your reputation, you have Expertise on social actions against those who know of you. Push Yourself to make your legend do the work — arriving at a crisis and having your presence alone resolve it.

**Duelist**
You have Expertise in single combat, formal challenges, and the culture of personal honor. Push Yourself to perform a Potent feat of swordsmanship or personal combat mastery.

### Mystic
**Prophetic Vision [ ] [ ]**
Mark this to ask the GM one question about what is coming — a danger ahead, a hidden threat, an outcome not yet determined. The GM answers honestly with what your patron reveals. The answer may be cryptic, but it is true.

**Rite of Passage**
You have Expertise when guiding others through significant life transitions — births, deaths, marriages, initiations, and moments of transformation. Push Yourself to perform a rite that changes someone's fundamental relationship with the world — binding an oath that cannot be broken, consecrating a hero's death, initiating someone into a spiritual tradition.

### Sage
**Eidetic Memory [ ] [ ]**
You have Expertise when recalling lore, languages, symbols, histories, and magical theory. Mark this to perfectly recall any detail your character previously witnessed — ask the GM one question, they answer honestly and completely.

**Perfect Execution [ ] [ ]**
Mark this when casting a known theorem. The casting requires no roll—treat the result as an automatic Conflict outcome (you succeed but face a Minor Consequence). You still pay any Stress costs if applicable.

**Runesmith**
You have Expertise when inscribing, reading, or modifying runes and magical glyphs. Push Yourself to inscribe a rune that lasts permanently or activates under specific conditions you define.

**Spell Turning**
You have Expertise when Defying hostile magic targeting you. Push Yourself to reflect a spell back at its caster with its original potency.

**Arcane Focus**
You have crafted or bonded with a magical focus (staff, wand, orb, etc.). When casting through your focus, you may treat 1 Bane as if it were not present. If your focus is lost or destroyed, you must spend a Long Rest creating or bonding with a new one.

### Hunter
**Patient Predator**
When you spend time observing a target, ask one: "What are they afraid of?" / "What are they protecting?" / "When are they most vulnerable?" The GM answers honestly. Push Yourself to act immediately on that information with Potent effect — no further setup required.

**Danger Sense**
You have Edge when keeping watch or detecting ambushes. When the GM uses Vigilance against you (Hint → Reveal → Strike), you always get at least a Reveal before Strike (never fully surprised by living creatures).

### Shadow
**Poisoner**
You have Expertise when crafting, identifying, or using poisons and toxins. Push Yourself to create a poison with a specific, unusual effect beyond simple harm — paralysis, truth serum, memory loss, sleep.

**Blackmailer**
You have Expertise when gathering compromising information or leveraging secrets against others. When you successfully investigate someone, ask: "What secret would ruin them?" The GM answers honestly.

**Killing Blow**
When you strike an unaware or helpless target with lethal intent, you may Push Yourself to kill them outright — no roll required. The GM may rule this ineffective against supernatural creatures, significantly more powerful foes, or targets with narrative protection.

### Vanguard
**War Cry**
When you Push Yourself in combat, you can unleash a battle cry that grants every nearby ally 1 Boon on their next action during this scene.

**Rally the Faithful**
You have Expertise when inspiring courage, discipline, loyalty, or unity. Push Yourself to rally an entire group instantly — turning rout into resolve.

**Living Fortress**
When given time, you can establish a defensible position — a fortification, barricade, or protective arrangement. You and your allies gain Expertise on all actions to defend this position. Push Yourself to establish one immediately, even in the middle of a scene.

**Last Line**
When an ally within Close range would be Taken Out, you may immediately Push Yourself to interpose — you suffer the consequence that would have taken them out instead. The GM describes how you shield them from the blow.

## Talent Design Patterns

### Standard Structure
Most talents follow: **Expertise in [domain] + Push Yourself for Potent [effect]**

### Checkbox Talents [ ] [ ]
- 2 uses per Short Rest
- Often grant free Push or Resist without Stress cost
- Examples: Battleborn, Defy Death, Relentless Pursuit, Beast Master

### Always-On Benefits
- Expertise grants (convert Setback → Conflict)
- Edge grants (ignore Complications)
- Conditional triggers (One Against Many, Indomitable)

### Push Yourself Options
- Perform Potent feat (exceeds normal limits)
- Act immediately (bypass time requirements)
- Extend effect to others (individual → group)
- Completely ignore/negate effect (partial → full)
