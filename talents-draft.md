# Talents Draft

## Champion

### Martial Prowess
You can Push Yourself to perform a Potent feat of combat skill, athleticism, or battlefield mastery.

### Battleborn [ ] [ ]
You have Expertise in feats of Athletics and Endurance. You can mark this to Push Yourself in combat without spending Stress, or to Resist a physical Consequence without spending Momentum.

### Weapon Mastery
You have Expertise with all weapons.

### Mighty
When given time, you can perform a Potent feat of raw strength or endurance — breaking barriers, moving impossible weights, enduring physical punishment. Push Yourself to do it immediately.

### Fearsome Presence
You have Expertise in intimidation, threats, and commanding through force of personality. You can Push Yourself to perform a Potent feat of intimidation.

### Iron Will
You have Expertise when Defying fear, coercion, temptation, or any attempt to break your resolve. Push Yourself to completely ignore an effect that would compel, dominate, or subvert your will.

## Hunter

### Master Tracker
You have Expertise when tracking creatures, reading signs, following trails, or locating prey. You can Push Yourself to perform a Potent feat of tracking, such as tracking something that should be impossible to follow.

### Monster Slayer
When you spend time studying a creature, ask: "What is its greatest weakness?"
The GM answers honestly. You can Push Yourself to exploit that weakness with Potent effect.

### Wilderness Survivor
You have Expertise in wilderness survival, navigation, and foraging. Push Yourself to endure or overcome natural conditions that would kill an ordinary person — extreme cold, starvation, toxic environments, days without rest.

### Deadeye
You have Expertise on precise ranged attacks. You can Push Yourself to perform a Potent feat of marksmanship — extreme distances, impossible angles, through superior cover, or against quickly moving targets.

### Relentless Pursuit [ ] [ ]
Mark this to immediately overcome an impediment — a barrier, a crowd, a distance, a locked door — preventing you from reaching your quarry. Nothing stops the hunt.

### Lightning Reflexes
You can always act first in a conflict, unless completely surprised. You also have Expertise when dodging, avoiding, or evading danger.

### Insightful
You have Expertise when reading people, detecting lies, or sensing hidden motives. When you successfully Sense someone's intentions, you also learn one thing they're trying to hide.

## Mystic

### Divine Favor
Your patron can act through you directly, not just alongside you. Push Yourself to perform a Potent miracle within your Domains — an effect beyond what your training or faith could normally achieve. The GM determines the exact manifestation based on your patron's nature.

### Sacred Authority
You have Expertise when invoking your patron, faith, spirits, or sacred office. You can Push Yourself to perform a Potent feat of command or exorcism.

### Miracle Worker
You can Push Yourself to perform Potent healing, purification, or restoration.

### Spirit Walker
You can perceive and communicate with beings beyond the mortal world — spirits, ancestors, echoes of the dead, entities that do not speak to ordinary mortals. Push Yourself to cross the boundary between worlds, entering a place or state that mortals cannot normally reach.

### Chosen Vessel [ ] [ ]
Mark this after rolling Setback on a patron invocation. Treat the result as Conflict instead. This conversion happens before Favor loss is assessed — if the converted result would not normally cause Favor loss, it doesn't.

## Sage

### Counter-Theory
You have Expertise when analyzing, dispelling, countering, or disrupting magic. You can Push Yourself to ignore magical protections or wards.

### Sphere Mastery
Choose a Sphere you know. You have Expertise whenever acting within that Sphere. Push Yourself to perform a Potent feat of magical theory within it — effects of unusual scale, precision, or permanence that exceed normal theorem limits.

### Arcane Engineer
You have Expertise when creating magical devices, wards, constructs, or enchantments. You can Push Yourself to rapidly improvise magical solutions.

### Efficient Casting [ ] [ ]
Mark this when casting a Potent Spell — whether from a known theorem or an Arcane Formula improvisation. The spell does not cost Stress. Boxes refresh on a short rest.

### Eidetic Memory [ ] [ ]
Mark this to perfectly recall specific information your character previously encountered — a map, a conversation, a symbol, a name, or any detail you witnessed. Ask the GM one question about what you remember. The GM answers truthfully and completely.

## Shadow

### Ghost
You have Expertise when hiding, infiltrating, escaping notice, or bypassing security. You can Push Yourself to disappear from a situation that should be impossible to escape.

### Master Deceiver
You can maintain deceptions that should be impossible to sustain — false identities for months, lies that survive magical scrutiny, facades that hold under direct interrogation. Push Yourself to perform a Potent feat of deception. You have Expertise in disguise, forgery, and constructed identities.

### Opportunist
When you exploit a weakness, distraction, or opening, you can perform an action that would normally require extensive setup — lifting a key mid-conversation, planting evidence during a search, slipping past a guard in the moment they look away.

### Slippery [ ] [ ]
Mark this to Resist a consequence caused by pursuit, detection, capture, or exposure.

### Ambush
When you act from hiding, spring a trap, or catch an enemy completely off-guard, your action is Potent.

---

## Vanguard

### Shield Wall
You have Expertise when protecting others from danger. You can Push Yourself to shield multiple allies at once.

### Stand Fast
You can hold ground that should be indefensible. Push Yourself to prevent a threat from advancing, breaching, or overwhelming a position entirely — buying time, holding a door, or refusing to yield no matter the odds. You have Expertise when holding ground or defending a fixed position.

### Guardian's Sacrifice [ ] [ ]
Mark this to suffer a consequence intended for an ally. Then reduce its severity by one step.

### Rally the Faithful
You have Expertise when inspiring courage, discipline, loyalty, or unity. You can Push Yourself to rally an entire group.

### Unmovable
When given time, you can establish a defensible position — a fortification, barricade, or protective arrangement. Push Yourself to establish one immediately, even in the middle of a scene.

### Tough as Nails
When you would mark Stress, mark 1 less (minimum 1).

### Immovable Object
You are immune to effects that would move you against your will or knock you prone.

## Design Notes

### Design Questions to Address:

**1. Potent Definition**
Needs half-page with examples across domains (combat, social, physical, magical). Based on Grimwild's Potency rules: ignore thorns from difficulty, attempt impossible (+3t) tasks.

**2. Guardian's Sacrifice as Core Talent**
Strong candidate - iconic, reactive, dramatic, clear. Consider replacing Iron Bulwark as Vanguard Core Talent.

**3. Clarifications Needed:**
- Martial Prowess vs Mighty distinction (combat feats vs raw strength?)
- Mystic talent scope overlap (Divine Favor, Sacred Authority, Miracle Worker)
- Relentless Pursuit: roll required or pure narrative permission?
- Guardian's Sacrifice: how to define "intended for an ally" vs area effects?

**4. Recent Changes:**
- Removed Dirty Tricks from Shadow (too similar to Champion's Martial Superiority Core Talent)
- Battleborn revised: Expertise in Athletics/Endurance + combat checkboxes for Push/Resist physical Consequences
- Added Weapon Mastery as separate talent (Expertise with all weapons)
- Added new talents: Lightning Reflexes, Insightful, Ambush, Eidetic Memory, Tough as Nails, Immovable Object

**5. Expertise Phrasing Conventions:**
- "in [skill/domain]" for broad capabilities (in Intimidation, in disguise/forgery)
- "with [tool/equipment]" for equipment-based (with all weapons)
- "when [condition/action]" for conditional triggers (when Defying fear, when tracking creatures)

### Talent Structure Patterns:

**Template 1: Expertise + Push for Potent**
Most common pattern. Always-on competence + dramatic escalation option.

**Template 2: Checkbox Resource**
Limited-use (2 boxes per rest) for clutch moments. Used sparingly (1 per Calling).

**Template 3: Narrative Permission**
Grant fictional capabilities (Opportunist, Spirit Walker). Creates story possibilities, not just mechanical bonuses.

**Template 4: Conditional/Gated Power**
"When given time" or "when [trigger]" for powerful effects with narrative cost.
