## Talents On Hold (Future Consideration)

### Champion
**Living Legend**
Your name and deeds precede you. When you reveal your identity or invoke your reputation, you have Expertise on social actions against those who know of you. Push Yourself to make your legend do the work — arriving at a crisis and having your presence alone resolve it.

**Duelist**
You have Expertise in single combat, formal challenges, and the culture of personal honor. Push Yourself to perform a Potent feat of swordsmanship or personal combat mastery.

### Mystic
**Prophetic Vision [ ] [ ]**
Mark this to ask the GM one question about what is coming — a danger ahead, a hidden threat, an outcome not yet determined. The GM answers honestly with what your patron reveals. The answer may be cryptic, but it is true.

**Rite of Passage**
You have Expertise when guiding others through significant life transitions — births, deaths, marriages, initiations, and moments of transformation. Push Yourself to perform a rite that changes someone's fundamental relationship with the world — binding an oath that cannot be broken, consecrating a hero's death, initiating someone into a spiritual tradition.

### Sage
**Eidetic Memory [ ] [ ]**
You have Expertise when recalling lore, languages, symbols, histories, and magical theory. Mark this to perfectly recall any detail your character previously witnessed — ask the GM one question, they answer honestly and completely.

**Perfect Execution [ ] [ ]**
Mark this when casting a known theorem. The casting requires no roll—treat the result as an automatic Conflict outcome (you succeed but face a Minor Consequence). You still pay any Stress costs if applicable.

**Runesmith**
You have Expertise when inscribing, reading, or modifying runes and magical glyphs. Push Yourself to inscribe a rune that lasts permanently or activates under specific conditions you define.

**Spell Turning**
You have Expertise when Defying hostile magic targeting you. Push Yourself to reflect a spell back at its caster with its original potency.

**Arcane Focus**
You have crafted or bonded with a magical focus (staff, wand, orb, etc.). When casting through your focus, you may treat 1 Bane as if it were not present. If your focus is lost or destroyed, you must spend a Long Rest creating or bonding with a new one.

### Hunter
**Patient Predator**
When you spend time observing a target, ask one: "What are they afraid of?" / "What are they protecting?" / "When are they most vulnerable?" The GM answers honestly. Push Yourself to act immediately on that information with Potent effect — no further setup required.

**Danger Sense**
You have Edge when keeping watch or detecting ambushes. When the GM uses Vigilance against you (Hint → Reveal → Strike), you always get at least a Reveal before Strike (never fully surprised by living creatures).

### Shadow
**Poisoner**
You have Expertise when crafting, identifying, or using poisons and toxins. Push Yourself to create a poison with a specific, unusual effect beyond simple harm — paralysis, truth serum, memory loss, sleep.

**Blackmailer**
You have Expertise when gathering compromising information or leveraging secrets against others. When you successfully investigate someone, ask: "What secret would ruin them?" The GM answers honestly.

**Killing Blow**
When you strike an unaware or helpless target with lethal intent, you may Push Yourself to kill them outright — no roll required. The GM may rule this ineffective against supernatural creatures, significantly more powerful foes, or targets with narrative protection.

### Vanguard
**War Cry**
When you Push Yourself in combat, you can unleash a battle cry that grants every nearby ally 1 Boon on their next action during this scene.

**Rally the Faithful**
You have Expertise when inspiring courage, discipline, loyalty, or unity. Push Yourself to rally an entire group instantly — turning rout into resolve.

**Living Fortress**
When given time, you can establish a defensible position — a fortification, barricade, or protective arrangement. You and your allies gain Expertise on all actions to defend this position. Push Yourself to establish one immediately, even in the middle of a scene.

**Last Line**
When an ally within Close range would be Taken Out, you may immediately Push Yourself to interpose — you suffer the consequence that would have taken them out instead. The GM describes how you shield them from the blow.

## Talent Design Patterns

### Standard Structure
Most talents follow: **Expertise in [domain] + Push Yourself for Potent [effect]**

### Checkbox Talents [ ] [ ]
- 2 uses per Short Rest
- Often grant free Push or Resist without Stress cost
- Examples: Battleborn, Defy Death, Relentless Pursuit, Beast Master

### Always-On Benefits
- Expertise grants (convert Setback → Conflict)
- Edge grants (ignore Complications)
- Conditional triggers (One Against Many, Indomitable)

### Push Yourself Options
- Perform Potent feat (exceeds normal limits)
- Act immediately (bypass time requirements)
- Extend effect to others (individual → group)
- Completely ignore/negate effect (partial → full)
